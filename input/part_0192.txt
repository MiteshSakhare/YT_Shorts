# Part 24: The Twice-Crowned King

NARRATOR:
He sat with her until the candles burned low, the silver ring on his finger pulsing with a light that was hers, that was Seraphina’s, that was Rin’s, that was his own.

The spring turned to summer, the summer to autumn, the autumn to winter. And in the winter, when the borderlands were white with snow and the Academy was quiet with the peace that had finally, truly come, Kaelen stood on the walls and watched the sun set over the world he had helped to build.

Seraphina came up behind him, her arms wrapping around his waist, her warmth seeping through his coat. Rin was on his left, her head on his shoulder, her eyes half-closed. Elara was on his right, her notebooks tucked under her arm, her voice soft as she read aloud from the history she was writing.

He did not rule. He did not conquer. He did not wear a crown.

But when he looked at the women beside him, at the lights of the Academy flickering below, at the stars coming out one by one in the darkening sky, he felt something he had never felt in three hundred years of empire.

He felt peace.

“What are you thinking?” Seraphina asked, her voice soft.

He looked at the silver ring on his finger, at the faces of the women he loved, at the world that was still being built, stone by stone, choice by choice.

“I’m thinking,” he said, “that this is what I was meant to be.”

He turned from the walls, and together they walked back into the light, the twice-crowned king and the women who had chosen him, the darkness in his chest a quiet heartbeat, the future waiting to be written.

End of Part 24
