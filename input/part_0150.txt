The attack came not at dawn, not at midnight, but in the quiet hour after lunch, when the Academy was at its most vulnerable.

Kaelen felt it before he heard it—a tremor in the wards that Elara had spent weeks reinforcing, a crack in the foundation that should not have been there. He was in the study with Seraphina, reviewing the council’s latest proposals, when the silver ring on his finger went cold.

He was on his feet before Seraphina could speak. “Something’s wrong.”

She was beside him instantly, her hand on her sword. “The wards?”

“Breached.” He was already moving, the power within him rising, the black streak in his hair darkening. “Get to the courtyard. Rally the defenders. I’ll find the source.”

She went, her footsteps echoing in the corridor, and he turned toward the source of the breach—the old ritual chamber beneath the Academy, the place where he had broken his chains.
