# Part 5: The Heretic’s Gambit

NARRATOR:
“You accuse me of being a heretic,” he said, his voice calm and clear. “On what evidence?”

The scarred cadet’s eyes narrowed. “The Inquisition does not answer to—”

“It answers to the truce,” Kaelen interrupted. “The same truce that allows your faction to exist within these walls. The same truce that says no student may be condemned without a tribunal of all three powers.” He held up the silver coin. “Your agent gave me this. He marked me without consent, without evidence, without trial. That alone is a violation of Academy charter. Every demon, every human, every Church member in this corridor is a witness.”

Whispers rippled through the crowd. The demon students straightened. Even some of the human cadets exchanged uncertain glances.

“You think words will protect you?” The scarred cadet drew a short sword from his belt—ceremonial, but the edge was real. “I have authority to detain any—”

“You have nothing.”

The voice came from behind the Church formation. The students parted, and Gaius walked through, his expression unreadable. The scarred cadet’s face twisted with surprise.

“Cadet Gaius. You’re the one who identified him.”

“I identified a potential anomaly,” Gaius said, stopping between Kaelen and the Church squad. “I did not authorize an armed incursion into the demon quarter. You’ve overstepped, Captain Valerius.”

Valerius’s jaw tightened. “The boy’s mana registered as corrupt. You said so yourself.”

“I said his mana was unusual.” Gaius’s pale eyes met Kaelen’s. “There is a difference. One that the Inquisition’s own protocols require a full investigation before action. You’ve given the demon faction a political weapon, Captain. Congratulations.”
