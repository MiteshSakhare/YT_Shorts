# Part 3: The Pawn’s First Move

NARRATOR:
Hair and the insignia of the Inquisition caught his eye. His gaze was cold, assessing, as if Kaelen had just been added to a very specific list.

After class, as Kaelen made his way toward the healers’ wing (a lie he intended to maintain for appearances), a hand caught his wrist. He was pulled into an alcove, his back against the cold stone, and found himself staring into a pair of fiery orange eyes.

Not Seraphina. A different demon girl—one he hadn’t noticed before. She was shorter than him, with wild auburn hair, small curved horns, and a freckled face that belied the predatory grin stretching across her lips. Her uniform was disheveled, her tie loose, as if she’d just been in a scuffle.

“You’re the Vane boy,” she said, her voice a husky whisper. “The one whose mana tastes like midnight and old empires.”

“I don’t know what you mean,” Kaelen replied, his meek mask firmly in place.

She laughed, a sound like breaking glass. “Oh, you’re good. But I can smell it on you. I’m a glutton demon, see. We don’t see mana. We taste it.” She leaned closer, her nose almost touching his neck. “And you, pretty boy, are the most delicious thing I’ve encountered in this entire boring Academy.”

Before he could respond, she pressed her lips to his throat—not a kiss, but a sip. A sharp sting, then a wave of dizziness. She pulled back, her eyes blazing with satisfaction.
