Rin found them an hour later, sprawled across a pile of cushions she’d dragged into the corner of the study. She took one look at Elara’s flushed face and Kaelen’s unbuttoned collar and grinned.

“Finally. I was starting to think you’d never make a move.”

Elara sat up, smoothing her hair. “It’s not—we weren’t—”

“It’s fine.” Rin dropped onto the cushions beside them, uninvited and unapologetic. “I don’t mind sharing. Seraphina’s the one who gets territorial.” She leaned into Kaelen’s side, her warmth seeping through his shirt. “Besides, I already had first taste. And second. And third.”

Elara’s eyes widened. “You’ve been counting?”

“Glutton demons count everything.” Rin’s grin softened into something more genuine. “He tastes like home. Did I ever tell you that? Like somewhere I’ve been looking for my whole life.”

Elara was quiet for a moment. Then she reached out and took Kaelen’s hand, her fingers interlacing with his. “He feels like a question I’ve been trying to answer since I was old enough to read.”

“He feels like a promise,” a new voice said.
