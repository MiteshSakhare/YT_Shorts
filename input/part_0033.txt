# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
“I wouldn’t miss it,” he said, his voice mild. “After all, I have so much to learn.”

Her smile sharpened. “Yes. You do.”

The head instructor—a massive demon with four arms and a bored expression—stepped forward. “Pairings are as follows: human faction paired with human faction. Demon faction paired with demon faction. Church faction paired with Church faction. No cross-faction spars without explicit faculty approval. Violations will result in immediate expulsion.”

A murmur of disappointment rippled through the crowd. Kaelen saw Valerius, the scarred Church captain, exchange a frustrated glance with his subordinates. Seraphina’s expression flickered—annoyance, quickly suppressed.

They’re trying to keep the peace, Kaelen realized. No cross-faction fighting means no incidents means no broken truce. The faculty is terrified of what happened last night repeating itself.

He was paired with a demon student he didn’t recognize—a wiry boy with insectile wings and a nervous twitch. The boy barely lasted thirty seconds before Kaelen’s controlled, minimal movements sent him sprawling.

The instructor nodded. “Adequate.”

Kaelen returned to the sidelines, where Seraphina was waiting. Her own spar had been a display of overwhelming force—she had dismantled three opponents in under a minute, her movements fluid and precise.

“You’re holding back,” she murmured as he approached.

“So are you.”

She laughed. “Of course. But you’re holding back more. I could see it. That last strike—you could have broken his ribs. Instead, you pulled it.”
