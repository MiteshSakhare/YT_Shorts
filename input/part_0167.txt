# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
The celebration lasted until dawn, but Kaelen slipped away before the wine ran dry.

He found himself on the Academy walls again, the silver ring cold against his finger, the darkness in his chest restless. Below, the lights of the neutral quarter flickered like earthbound stars—students from all three factions dancing together, laughing together, building something fragile and new.

But beyond the walls, in the borderlands, there was only shadow.

“You’re thinking about what comes next.”

He didn’t turn. Vex’ahlia’s voice was familiar now, her presence a comfort he hadn’t expected. She moved to stand beside him, her bare feet silent on the stone, her silver hair loose in the night wind.

“Valerius was a symptom,” he said. “The corruption in his sword was old. Older than the truce. Older than the Church.” He looked at her. “You said it was mine. The part of me I thought I’d left behind.”

“I said it was the same darkness your empire was built on. I didn’t say it was yours.” She leaned against the parapet, her tarnished-gold eyes fixed on the horizon. “The sword wasn’t Valerius’s doing. Someone gave it to him. Someone who knew exactly what he would do with it.”

Kaelen’s jaw tightened. “Who?”

“I don’t know. But I’ve been doing some digging.” She reached into her tunic and produced a small scroll, its edges frayed, its ink faded. “This came from the Church’s deepest archives. It’s a record of the original pact—the one your soul made with Seraphina’s grandmother.”
