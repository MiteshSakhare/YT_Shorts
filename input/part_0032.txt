# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
Balcony overlooking the courtyard, a figure in white and silver watched him. A girl, younger than him, with platinum hair and eyes the color of winter sky. She wore the robes of a Church acolyte, but her posture was wrong—too relaxed, too confident, too knowing.

She smiled when their eyes met, and raised one finger to her lips.

Then she was gone, disappearing back into the shadows of the Church quarter.

Kaelen continued walking, his expression unchanged, but his mind was racing. A fourth faction? A fifth player? The Church had already tried to take him once. Was this girl with the winter eyes friend or foe?

He reached the training grounds, where students were already gathering in clusters—humans on one side, demons on the other, Church cadets in a separate formation. The instructors were a mix of all three factions, their expressions carefully neutral.

And there, at the center of the demon formation, stood Seraphina. She had changed into combat leathers that hugged her figure, her silver-blue hair tied back, her horns gleaming in the morning light. When she saw him, she smiled—the same warm, terrifying smile from the night before.

“Cousin,” she called, loud enough for everyone to hear. “I’m glad you came. I was worried you might sleep through the fun.”

The human students snickered. The demons watched with interest. The Church cadets stared with cold, assessing eyes.

Kaelen walked toward her, the crowd parting around him. He stopped a few feet away, close enough to see the faint sigil glowing on her palm—the one that matched his seal.
