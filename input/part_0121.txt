# Part 16: The First Stone

NARRATOR:
"We're outnumbered," Kaelen said, his voice carrying across the courtyard. "Outgunned. Outmatched. If we meet them on the field, we lose." He let the words hang, let the fear settle in their chests. "So we don't meet them on the field. We make them fight on our ground. In our shadows. At our pace. We don't defeat their army. We make them question why they came."

He walked among them, meeting their eyes, letting them see him—not the Demon Emperor, not the twice-crowned king, but a man who had been where they were, who had felt the same fear, who had chosen to be something other than what the world expected.

"The king's soldiers are not our enemies," he said. "They're farmers, blacksmiths, sons and daughters who were told that we are monsters. They're fighting for their homes, their families, their faith. We don't kill them. We show them the truth. We make them see that the real monster is the war itself."

He stopped at the center of the courtyard, turning to face them all. "Tomorrow, I will walk out to meet the king. I will give him one last chance to turn back. And if he doesn't…" He let the power within him rise, just enough to make the torches flicker, to make the ground tremble, to remind them that the man standing before them was more than he seemed. "If he doesn't, then I will show him exactly what he was so afraid of."

He looked at Seraphina. She nodded, her hand finding his.

He looked at Rin. She winked, her grin fierce.

He looked at Elara. She smiled, small and steady.
