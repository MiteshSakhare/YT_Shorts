# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
Valerius hissed, pressing the advantage. “The darkness you tried to hide. The hunger you tried to bury. You think you’ve changed, but you’re still what you always were. A monster wearing a boy’s face.”

Kaelen held the blade, his palms burning, the black light and black steel grinding against each other. “You’re wrong.”

“Am I?” Valerius twisted the sword, and Kaelen felt the corruption bite into his skin—not cutting, but feeding, drinking from the power within him, growing stronger with each drop. “The darkness doesn’t lie. It knows what you are. It knows what you’ll become.”

The silver ring on Kaelen’s finger blazed. Not with power—with light, pure and bright, the light of a promise made and kept. It surged through him, meeting the corruption, pushing it back, and for a moment, Kaelen saw Valerius as he truly was: a man hollowed out by hatred, filled with something that was not his own, a puppet dancing on strings he couldn’t see.

“Who gave you that sword?” Kaelen demanded. “Who sent you here?”

Valerius’s face twisted. “No one sent me. I came to finish what I started. To cleanse this place of your heresy.”

“You came to die.” Kaelen’s voice was cold, the Demon Emperor’s voice, the voice that had broken armies. “The sword is using you. The moment you strike, it will consume you. You know that. You’ve always known.”

For a moment, something flickered in Valerius’s eyes—recognition, perhaps, or the last spark of the man he had been before the hatred consumed him. Then it was gone, replaced by the same fervor, the same madness.
