# Part 15: The Throne of Embers

NARRATOR:
Hard, just enough to still her hand. “And if I don’t want to be written?”

Her breath caught. “Then write yourself.”

He pulled her closer, and she came willingly, her hands finding his shoulders, her face inches from his. The air between them was thick with something that had been building since the first night she’d touched the frost on his window.

“Elara.” His voice was low. “If you want something, you need to say it. I’m tired of guessing.”

Her cheeks flushed, but her eyes didn’t waver. “I want to be part of this. Whatever this is. I want to be close enough to see what you become. I want…” She swallowed. “I want to be one of the people you come home to. Not just an observer. Not just a scholar. Someone you want.”

He looked at her—really looked. The intelligence in her eyes, the tension in her jaw, the way her hands trembled slightly against his shoulders. She had given him the truth about the seal when no one else could. She had stood beside him when the Church came for him. She had risked everything for a boy she’d known for weeks, because she believed he could be something more.

He pulled her down, and she came with a soft gasp, her lips meeting his.

It was different from kissing Seraphina—less fire, more warmth. Different from Rin—less hunger, more depth. Elara kissed like she did everything: deliberately, thoughtfully, as if she was memorizing the shape of him for future reference.
