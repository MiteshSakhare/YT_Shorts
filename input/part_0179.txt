# Part 23: The Oracle’s Truth

NARRATOR:
Kaelen fought. He pushed against the darkness, tried to pull it back, to contain it the way he had contained Valerius’s corruption. But it was too strong, too old, too hungry. It rose through him like floodwater, drowning his will, his hope, his love.

And in the flood, he saw her.

Not the Oracle. A woman in white robes, her face obscured, her voice a whisper. Seraphina’s grandmother. The one who had made the pact.

“You promised me,” she whispered. “You promised me an heir. A king. A future. But you were afraid. You were always afraid. So you ran, and you left me with half your soul, half your darkness, half your curse.”

The vision shifted. He saw the darkness split, half sealed in his chest, half set free to wander. He saw the Oracle as she had been—a young priestess, faithful, devoted, who had found the prophecy and thought she was serving God. He saw her find the wandering darkness, mistake it for divine light, and bind herself to it.

He saw her waiting. For three hundred years, she had waited for the two halves to reunite. For the twice-crowned king to become what he was always meant to be.

The Unmaker, the darkness whispered. The end of all things. The final war that will burn the world clean.

Kaelen fell to his knees, the black light pouring from him, the silver ring on his finger flickering, dying. The Oracle stood over him, her face radiant with triumph.

“It’s time,” she said. “Let it go. Let the darkness consume you. Let it become what it was always meant to be.”
