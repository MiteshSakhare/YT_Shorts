The order rippled through the camp like a wave, disbelief giving way to relief, relief giving way to something that might have been gratitude. Soldiers lowered their weapons. Officers sheathed their swords. The great engines of war began to turn, not toward the Academy, but away, their purpose suddenly, impossibly, ended.

Aldric looked at Kaelen one last time. "I don't understand you."

"You don't have to." Kaelen turned, walking back toward the Academy gates, the dawn breaking through the clouds behind him. "Just remember what happened here. Remember that a heretic walked into your camp and walked out again, and the only thing he took from you was a sword that should never have been drawn."

He walked through the gates, and they closed behind him, and the army of the human king began its long march home.

Seraphina was waiting for him in the courtyard, her hands shaking, her face wet with tears she didn't bother to hide. She threw herself into his arms, and he held her, her heart pounding against his chest, her breath warm against his neck.

"You're insane," she whispered. "You're absolutely, completely insane."

"You're insane." She pulled back, her golden eyes blazing. "You walked into an army. You disarmed a king. You—" She stopped, her voice breaking. "You came back."

"I promised I would."

She kissed him, fierce and desperate, and he kissed her back, the power within him settling, the frozen ocean finally, fully, thawing.

When they broke apart, Rin was there, her grin wide, her eyes bright. "That was the most insane thing I've ever seen. And I've seen a lot of insane things."
