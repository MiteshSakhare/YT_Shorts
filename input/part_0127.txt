# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
And at the center, Vex'ahlia stood with her sword raised, the silver light of the Church's own wards reflecting off her blade, her tarnished-gold eyes fixed on the king's army.

"You want a war?" Vex'ahlia's voice carried across the no-man's-land, clear and cold. "Then know this: the Academy is not yours to take. The truce was broken by your captain, not by us. And the heretic you came to burn has done more to protect your people in a week than you have in your whole reign."

She pointed her sword at the king, who was struggling to his feet, his officers rushing to surround him. "Stand down, Aldric. Or learn what it means to face an enemy who fights for something other than pride."

The army wavered. The soldiers looked at their king, at the walls, at the light that still crackled on the battlements. They had been told they were coming to crush a rebellion, to destroy a heretic, to reclaim what was theirs. But the heretic stood before them with empty hands, and the walls they were meant to burn glowed with a light that was not demonic, not corrupt, but something that looked, impossibly, like faith.

Kaelen turned back to the king. Aldric was on his feet now, his face white, his hand on his sword. Around him, his officers were shouting, drawing their own weapons, forming a wall of steel between their king and the man who had walked into their camp alone.

"Your Majesty." Kaelen's voice was quiet, but it cut through the chaos like a blade. "This is your last chance. Call off the attack. Let the truce be rebuilt. Let your soldiers go home to their families. Let this end without bloodshed."
