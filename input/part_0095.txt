# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
“I know.” He brushed a strand of hair from her face. “I’m still me. I chose to be.”

Her breath caught. Then she laughed—a shaky, relieved sound—and threw her arms around him. He held her, the power within him settling, the river finding its banks.

The chamber door burst open. Rin stood in the entrance, a silver key in her hand, her face flushed with triumph. Behind her, Elara was gathering her books, her hands trembling, her eyes fixed on Kaelen with an intensity that bordered on reverence.

“Got it,” Rin said, then stopped. Her eyes traveled to the collar on the floor, to the broken seal on Kaelen’s chest, to the fading frost on the walls. “You did it.”

“We did it.” He stood, Seraphina still leaning against him. “All of us.”

Rin crossed the room in three strides and threw herself at him, her arms wrapping around his neck, her face buried in his shoulder. She was shaking, he realized. The glutton demon who had never shown fear was trembling against him.

“I could feel it,” she whispered. “When the seal broke. The power. It was like the whole world was screaming.” She pulled back, her orange eyes bright. “You came back.”

“I never left.”

She kissed him—hard, desperate, tasting of salt and magic—and he let her, one arm around Seraphina, one hand in Rin’s hair, the power within him humming in answer to both of them.
