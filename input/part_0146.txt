# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
Seraphina looked at him for a long moment, her golden eyes searching. “You really believe that, don’t you?”

“I have to. If I don’t, then what was any of this for?”

She kissed him then, soft and slow, and he let himself sink into her warmth, into the quiet certainty of her presence.

When they broke apart, she was smiling. “You’re a fool, Kaelen Vane.”

“A beautiful, idealistic fool.” She traced the black streak in his hair. “And I wouldn’t have you any other way.”

The first crisis came three weeks after the council convened.

A Church outpost on the border was attacked. Not by demons—the survivors were adamant about that. Human soldiers, wearing the colors of the king, had come in the night and burned the chapel to the ground. Three priests dead. A dozen wounded. And on the walls, scrawled in blood: THE HERETIC WILL BURN.

Kaelen received the news in the council chamber, the delegates gathered around him, their faces a mixture of fear and fury.

“This is a provocation,” Lord Malachar growled. “Someone wants to break the council before it can solidify.”

“Valerius,” Lady Ashford said, her voice tight. “He’s been recruiting in the borderlands. Men who followed the king’s army home and found nothing to return to.”

Mara’s masked face was unreadable. “The Church will investigate. If Valerius is behind this—”

“If?” Seraphina’s voice was sharp. “He’s declared himself. He’s calling for war. How long before others join him?”
