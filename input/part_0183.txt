# Part 23: The Oracle’s Truth

NARRATOR:
They looked at him, at the Oracle, at each other. And one by one, they began to move—not toward him, not away, but toward each other. A woman in the remnants of Church armor reached out to a man in the gray of the borderlands. A young soldier who had followed Valerius knelt beside an old priest who had lost his faith. They did not embrace. They did not weep. They simply stood together, in the gray light of dawn, and began the long, slow work of becoming human again.

Kaelen watched them, the darkness in his chest finally, fully, quiet. The silver ring on his finger pulsed with a light that was not his alone—it was Seraphina’s light, Rin’s light, Elara’s light, Vex’ahlia’s light. The light of everyone who had chosen to stand beside him, to believe in him, to love him.

He touched the ring, felt it warm against his skin, and smiled.

“Let’s go home,” he said.

End of Part 23
