# Part 16: The First Stone

NARRATOR:
Looking down at the herald. "Tell your king that the Academy is not his to take. Tell him that the truce was broken by his own man, Captain Valerius, who fled rather than face the consequences. Tell him that the heretic he seeks is standing here, and that I will meet him on the field of battle if he wants me—but not before every innocent in this Academy has been given the chance to leave."

The herald's face tightened. "You defy the king's command?"

"I defy a war that was started by liars." Kaelen's voice was calm, carrying across the walls, across the no-man's-land, to the edges of the human camp. "Tell Aldric that I will meet him at dawn. One man against his army. If he wants his war, I'll give it to him. But he'll have to face me first."

The herald stared up at him, and for a moment, something flickered in his eyes—not fear, but recognition. The recognition of a man who had just realized that the heretic he'd been sent to capture was not what he expected.

He turned his horse and rode back to the camp, the banner of truce still flying.

That night, Kaelen stood in the courtyard, the defenders gathered around him.

Seraphina was at his right hand, her armor gleaming, her golden eyes fierce. Rin was at his left, her dagger sheathed, her grin sharp. Elara stood behind them, her books abandoned, a sword she barely knew how to hold at her hip. Vex'ahlia was in the shadows, watching, waiting.

And around them, the students—human, demon, Church—stood in the torchlight, their faces young, their hands trembling, their hearts full of something that might have been hope.
