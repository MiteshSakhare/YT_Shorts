The victims’ hearings took three days.

One by one, the people Valerius had wronged came forward. Students who had lost friends in the purge. Church acolytes whose faith had been shattered by the violence. Demon families who had fled their homes in the borderlands. Each story was a wound, each testimony a scar.

Valerius listened to all of them. He did not speak, did not defend, did not look away. He stood in his chains and let their words wash over him, and by the end of the third day, his face was gray, his hands shaking, his eyes hollow.

When the last witness had spoken, the delegates withdrew to deliberate. Kaelen waited in the corridor, Seraphina beside him, the silver ring on his finger pulsing with a slow, steady rhythm.

“What do you think they’ll decide?” Seraphina asked.

“I don’t know.” He looked at the closed doors of the council chamber. “That’s the point. If I could predict it, it wouldn’t be justice. It would be my judgment.”

She leaned into him, her warmth seeping through his coat. “You’ve changed so much. Sometimes I forget that the man who walked into my family’s pact three hundred years ago is the same person standing here.”

“I’m not the same person.”
