# Part 24: The Twice-Crowned King

NARRATOR:
He looked at her—this woman who had waited three hundred years, who had bound him and freed him, who had chosen to stand beside him when she could have chosen power.

“I love you,” he said. It was the first time he had said the words, in this life or any other. They felt like a key turning in a lock, like a door opening onto something he had never let himself want.

She smiled, and it was the smile of a woman who had been waiting to hear those words for three hundred years. “I know.”

She kissed him, soft and slow, and the silver ring pulsed with a light that was theirs alone.

Later, when the celebration had quieted, Kaelen found Rin sitting on the old walls, her legs dangling over the edge, her face turned toward the stars.

“You’re not stealing food?”

“I’m full.” She patted her stomach, her grin lazy. “First time in my life. Your darkness tastes like dawn, did I tell you that? Like something new. I don’t think I’ll ever be hungry again.”

He sat beside her, his shoulder brushing hers. “That’s not how glutton demons work.”

“Maybe not.” She leaned into him, her warmth seeping through his coat. “But I’m not just a glutton demon anymore. I’m Rin. The woman who loves the twice-crowned king. The woman who tasted his darkness and made it her own.” She looked up at him, her orange eyes bright. “That’s enough for me.”

He put his arm around her, pulling her close. “It’s enough for me too.”
