# Part 15: The Throne of Embers

NARRATOR:
Her silver-blue hair loose, her golden eyes fixed on the three of them. She was wearing a simple gown—no armor, no adornment—and for once, she looked less like a queen and more like a woman who had been carrying a weight for three hundred years.

“A promise my grandmother made. A promise he broke. A promise that brought us all here.” She walked into the room, her steps slow, deliberate. “I spent my whole life preparing to collect that promise. To bind him, use him, make him pay for what he did.” She stopped beside the cushions, looking down at them. “And then I met him. And I realized I didn’t want a weapon. I didn’t want a prisoner. I wanted…”

She sat down on Kaelen’s other side, her shoulder brushing his. “I wanted this. All of this. The chaos. The danger. The people who look at him and see something worth fighting for.”

Rin snorted. “That’s very poetic for someone who tried to put a leash on him.”

“I changed.” Seraphina’s voice was sharp, but there was no heat in it. “I grew up. I saw what happens when you try to own something instead of earning it.” She looked at Kaelen. “My grandmother bound you. I want you to choose me. That’s the difference.”

Kaelen looked at the three of them—Rin warm against his side, Elara’s hand in his, Seraphina’s shoulder pressed against his—and felt the weight of what they were offering. Not power. Not a kingdom. A life. A future. Something he had never had, in any of his lives.
