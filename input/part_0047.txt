# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
“I deny that ‘corrupt’ is an accurate description.” He let his gaze sweep the chamber, meeting the eyes of each faction in turn. “My mana is unusual. It is not corrupt. The healer who examined me confirmed that my channels are simply… different.”

“Different,” the demon judge repeated, his sneer deepening. “Your power manifested as black light and frost in the middle of a training ground. Students were injured. The truce was nearly broken. That is not ‘different.’ That is dangerous.”

“The truce was nearly broken by Captain Valerius’s armed incursion into the demon quarter last night,” Kaelen said, his voice calm. “Not by my actions today. And the only student injured was injured by another student—a demon girl acting in self-defense after a Church cadet escalated a sparring match into an assault.”

Whispers rippled through the gallery. The human judge’s eyes narrowed. “You are not here to discuss Captain Valerius’s actions.”

“I am here to establish context,” Kaelen replied. “An accusation of heresy cannot be separated from the political climate that produced it. I was marked without my consent by a Church agent. I was targeted by an armed squad before any investigation was conducted. And now I stand before a tribunal that includes the very faction that has already declared me guilty.” He looked directly at the masked inquisitor. “Tell me, Inquisitor. When did the Church decide I was a heretic? Was it when my mana tested unusual? Or was it when I refused to be taken in chains?”
