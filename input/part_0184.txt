# Part 24: The Twice-Crowned King

NARRATOR:
Kaelen walked out of the gates of Blackthorn.

Behind him, the fortress stood transformed. The black stone walls still rose from the ash and thorn, but the darkness that had clung to them was gone, replaced by a light that seemed to come from within the rock itself. The followers of the Oracle had begun the work of dismantling the shrines, the banners, the symbols of the Unmaking. They would not return to the Academy—not yet—but they had chosen to stay, to rebuild, to turn a place of war into something that might, in time, become a home.

The Oracle herself had remained. When Kaelen had asked if she would come with him, she had shaken her head, her winter-sky eyes clear for the first time in three hundred years.

“I have been a vessel for darkness long enough,” she had said. “Now I must learn to be human again. There is work here—the work of healing. It is work I can do.”

He had left her at the gates, a small figure in gray robes, already surrounded by the people she had once led to war. She was not redeemed. She was not forgiven. But she was, for the first time, free.
