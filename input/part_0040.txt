# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
Rin’s hand moved to her hip, where a concealed dagger was likely hidden. “He’s busy.”

“He’s in danger,” Gaius said flatly. “The Church faction is convening an emergency tribunal. Captain Valerius is pushing for your immediate detention and interrogation. They’re claiming your display was a violation of the truce—an act of aggression by a known heretic.”

Kaelen stood, ignoring Rin’s protest. “When?”

“Tonight. After sundown.” Gaius’s jaw tightened. “I’ve argued against it, but Valerius has the votes. Unless someone with higher authority intervenes, you’ll be in a holding cell by midnight.”

“And who has higher authority?”

“The Inquisition’s regional commander. A woman named Vex’ahlia.” Gaius’s expression flickered—something that might have been fear or disgust. “She arrived this morning. Someone reported your display to the Church’s high council within minutes of it happening.”

Kaelen’s mind raced. Less than two hours ago. Someone was waiting for me to slip. Someone wanted this.

He looked at Gaius. “Take me to her.”

Vex’ahlia was not what he expected.

The Church’s regional commander was a woman of indeterminate age, with silver hair cropped short and eyes the color of tarnished gold. She wore no uniform, no robes—just a simple gray tunic and trousers, her feet bare on the cold stone floor of her temporary office. A single candle burned on the desk between them, casting her face in sharp shadows.

She was also, Kaelen realized with a jolt, the girl from the balcony. The one with winter eyes and the finger pressed to her lips.

“You’re younger than I expected,” she said, her voice soft, almost musical. “The reports said a boy of sixteen. I thought it was exaggeration.”

“I am sixteen.”
