# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
The masked inquisitor leaned forward. "The limiter is non-negotiable. It will be placed by Church artisans, keyed to the seal on his chest. It will not harm him. It will not restrict his daily activities. It will simply ensure that the black resonance does not endanger the Academy again."

Seraphina stood. "And who holds the key?"

The demon judge answered. "The key will be held by a representative of each faction—one human, one demon, one Church. No single faction can activate or remove the limiter without the others' consent."

Seraphina's eyes met Kaelen's across the hall. He saw the calculation in her gaze—this was acceptable. The limiter was a leash, yes, but it was a leash shared by all three factions. That meant no single faction could use it against him without breaking the truce. It was a cage, but it was a cage with three locks, and that made it a prison that could become a weapon.

He nodded, once.

The human judge struck her gavel. "The limiter will be fitted immediately. Kaelen Vane, you are dismissed."

The fitting chamber was small, windowless, lit by a single brazier of silver flame.

Three artisans waited for him: a human woman with hands like a surgeon, a demon with four arms and jeweler's tools, and a Church priest whose face was hidden behind a white cloth mask. They worked in silence, measuring, calibrating, the limiter taking shape between them—a collar of interlocking rings, silver and black, each ring inscribed with a different faction's sigil.
