# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
Dawn came quietly, pale light seeping through the dormitory window like a held breath.

Kaelen woke to warmth. Rin was still pressed against his side, her head on his chest, her hair a wild tangle of auburn across his shoulder. One of her legs had hooked over his during the night, and her hand lay flat against his sternum, directly over the seal. She was breathing softly, her lips parted, her expression peaceful in a way he hadn't seen before.

He should move. Should disentangle himself before the guards changed shift, before the Academy's gossip mill turned this into another weapon to use against him. But the seal was quiet beneath her palm, and for once, the frozen ocean within him was still.

When was the last time I slept without dreaming? He couldn't remember. In his past life, sleep had been a battlefield—visions of conquest and betrayal, the faces of those he'd destroyed. In this life, the dreams had been Seraphina's territory, her voice winding through his subconscious like ivy through ruins.

But last night, with Rin's heartbeat against his ribs, there had been nothing. Just darkness. Just rest.

He looked at her face, really looked. The freckles scattered across her nose. The small scar on her chin, hidden by the curve of her jaw. The way her lips twitched in sleep, as if she was tasting something even in dreams.

A glutton demon, he thought. Born to consume. And she chooses to sleep beside me like a cat in a sunbeam.

Her eyes opened.
