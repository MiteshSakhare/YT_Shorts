# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
“I believe it’s the only chance we have.” He looked at each of them in turn. “The demon lords want security. The human king wants stability. The Church wants to preserve its faith. None of those things are mutually exclusive. But they require something none of you have been willing to give before.”

“And what is that?” Lord Malachar asked.

“Trust.” Kaelen’s voice was quiet, but it filled the room. “You don’t have to like each other. You don’t have to agree on everything. But you have to be willing to sit at the same table and talk instead of fight. That’s what this council is. A table. The rest is up to you.”

Lady Ashford’s eyes narrowed. “And what is your role in this council, Lord Vane? You speak of equals, yet you stand at the center.”

“I’m not a lord. I’m not a king. I’m not a delegate.” He spread his hands. “I’m the one who stopped the war. That gives me the right to ask you to try something new. It doesn’t give me the right to rule. I won’t sit on this council. I won’t hold a vote. I’ll be here when you need me, and I’ll step back when you don’t.”

The silence that followed was thick with disbelief. Lord Malachar’s scales rippled. Lady Ashford’s composure cracked. Even Mara seemed taken aback.

“You’re refusing power,” Malachar said slowly. “After everything you’ve done to gain it.”

“I’m refusing a throne. There’s a difference.” Kaelen smiled, thin and tired. “I spent three hundred years on a throne. It was the loneliest place in the world. I’m not doing that again.”
