# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
His wife. And neither of them knows it yet.

Kaelen's hands tightened on the notebook. A marriage. Seraphina called it an investment, a partnership, a bloodline bond. But Elara had found the truth: the seal wasn't just a leash. It was a wedding ring.

He closed the notebook and tucked it into his coat. Elara had left it for him deliberately—a gift, a warning, a declaration of her own value. I know the truth, it said. And I'll share it with you, if you let me close enough.

Another player, he thought. Another person who wants a piece of me.

But as he walked toward the main hall, the guards flanking him, the notebook pressed against his ribs, he couldn't bring himself to feel the cold calculation he'd worn as armor in his past life. The pieces were moving too fast, the players too close. Seraphina wanted to bind him. Rin wanted to taste him. Elara wanted to understand him. And somewhere in the shadows, Vex'ahlia was watching, waiting for him to tip.

They're not pieces on a board, he realized. They're people. And I'm not sure anymore if I want to move them or let them move me.

The main hall was packed.

Every student who could fit had crammed into the tiered seating, their faces a mosaic of anticipation and fear. The faculty occupied a raised platform at the far end, the three judges seated in the center, Vex'ahlia standing behind them like a shadow given form.
