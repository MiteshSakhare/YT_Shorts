# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
She slid into the chair across from him, her dark curls escaping from a messy bun, her eyes shadowed with exhaustion. A stack of books towered beside her, their spines cracked, their pages bristling with markers.

"You've been reading," she said, her voice quiet.

"So have you."

She pulled one of the books toward her, opening it to a page marked with a ribbon. "I found something. About the seal. About what Morwen said—that it's become something new." She pushed the book across the table. "There's a theory in old demonic texts about something called a soul-bond. It's not supposed to be possible. It requires two souls that are… compatible. Resonant. Like two instruments that play the same note."

Kaelen looked at the page. The text was ancient, the script looping and unfamiliar, but Elara had translated it in neat handwriting along the margins:

The soul-bond cannot be forced. It can only be recognized. When two souls that were once one are reunited, the bond forms naturally, drawing them together across any distance, any lifetime. It is not a cage. It is a homecoming.

He looked up. "You think Seraphina and I are soul-bonded?"

"I think the seal was meant to bind you to her bloodline. Instead, it bound you to her. To Rin. To me." She met his eyes, and for the first time, he saw something in her gaze that wasn't academic curiosity. "To everyone who's touched you since you woke in this body. You're not just collecting us, Kaelen. We're collecting you."
