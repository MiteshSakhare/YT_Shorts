# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
“You’ve just painted a target on your back the size of the Academy.” She released his hand, but stayed close, her shoulder brushing his. “Every faction will want you now. The Church will demand you be tested, catalogued, controlled. The human nobles will see you as either a weapon or a threat. And my demon lords…” She smiled, thin and sharp. “They’ll want to know why a half-blood nobody just manifested power that rivals a high lord’s.”

“Then I suppose it’s a good thing I have you to manage the politics.”

She laughed, and this time it was genuine. “Oh, you’ll be managing plenty yourself. I’m not your nursemaid, Emperor. I’m your partner. That means you carry your share of the weight.”

She turned to leave, then paused, glancing back over her shoulder. “One piece of advice. The demon lords will send someone to evaluate you. Soon. Probably within the week. When they come, don’t hide. Don’t be meek. Show them what you showed me today.” Her golden eyes gleamed. “Show them why I chose you.”

She walked away, her combat leathers hugging the curve of her hips, her tail swaying lazily behind her. A dozen demon students fell into step behind her, their glances at Kaelen newly respectful—or newly wary.

He stood alone in the empty arena, frost melting around his feet, and let out a slow breath.

She chose me. But she didn’t tell me what the evaluation would cost.

The mess hall at noon was a gauntlet.
