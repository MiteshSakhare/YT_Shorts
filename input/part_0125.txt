# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
Officers. Kaelen saw it—the first crack of doubt.

Aldric's jaw tightened. "Valerius's absence proves nothing. You could have killed him. You could have—"

"I could have done many things." Kaelen's power rose again, not threatening, but present—a reminder of what he was, of what he had chosen not to be. "I could have burned this Academy to the ground the moment your captain attacked. I could have called the demon lords to war. I could have met your army with an army of my own, and the borderlands would be running with blood by now."

He spread his hands, empty, open. "Instead, I opened the gates. I let anyone leave who wanted to. I sent your soldiers back to you, unharmed, with nothing but a message: this war is not necessary. This war is not just. This war was built on lies by a man who wanted power, not peace."

He met Aldric's eyes. "You are the Lion of the South. Your people call you the Bold. They say you are a man of honor, a man who fights for what is right. Prove them right. Call off this attack. Let the Academy stand. Let the truce be rebuilt, not on fear, but on truth."

For a long moment, Aldric said nothing. The camp was silent, ten thousand soldiers holding their breath, waiting for their king to speak.

Then Aldric laughed.
