# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
He thought of the frozen ocean within him. The ice. The chains. The darkness coiled beneath.

No holding back.

He placed his palm flat against the crystal.

The cold hit him first—a cold that had nothing to do with temperature, a cold that came from the deepest part of the seal, from the frozen ocean that had been waiting for three hundred years. The obelisk drank it in, its facets flickering, shifting from black to silver to something that had no name.

And then the memories came.

He saw himself as he had been: the Demon Emperor, standing on a mountain of corpses, his crimson eyes burning with a hunger that could never be sated. He saw the Crystal Court fall, its queen’s last scream echoing through the halls. He saw the Sunken City burning, the waters boiling, the souls of the drowned rising like steam.

He saw the woman in white robes—her face still obscured, her voice still a whisper—and he heard himself speak the words of the pact.

“In exchange for salvation, I give you my bloodline. My heir. My throne. All that I am, I bind to you and yours. Until death. Until the end of days.”

He saw himself running. Saw the chains of light wrapping around his heart as he fled, the pact half-fulfilled, the promise broken. Saw the woman’s face, finally clear, as he turned away.

She had Seraphina’s eyes. Seraphina’s smile. Seraphina’s fury.

“You will return,” she whispered. “In this life or the next. And when you do, I will make you keep your word.”

The memory shattered. Kaelen pulled his hand from the obelisk, gasping, the cold receding from his bones.

The crystal blazed.
