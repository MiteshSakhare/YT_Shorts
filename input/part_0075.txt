# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
He looked at his reflection in the obelisk’s silver-black surface. A boy with white hair and violet eyes. A collar around his neck. A seal on his chest. A frozen ocean inside him, waiting to thaw.

Let them stand beside me, he thought. Let them all come. Let them see what rises.

He turned and walked off the platform, his steps steady, his head high.

Behind him, the obelisk continued to glow—silver and black, light and dark, a new creation born from old ruins.

End of Part 10

The days that followed the evaluation were a strange, suspended calm—the quiet before a storm that everyone could feel but no one could name.

Kaelen moved through the Academy like a ghost with too many shadows. The collar around his neck marked him as contained, but the silver-black light of the obelisk had branded him as something more. Students parted when he walked through corridors. Instructors called on him less frequently. Even the Church cadets, who had once watched him with open hostility, now averted their eyes when he passed.

Fear, he thought, sitting in the back of Political Doctrine while Professor Halsten droned about the economic foundations of the truce. They don't know what I am, so they fear me. Better than love. Easier to weaponize.

But not everyone was afraid.

Rin had taken to appearing at his side at odd hours—during meals, between classes, once in the middle of a mana theory lecture, slipping through a window to drop into the seat beside him with a grin that made the instructor choke on his chalk. She didn't ask permission. She didn't explain. She simply was, her presence a warm, hungry constant that the seal seemed to welcome.
