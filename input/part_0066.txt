# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
Morning arrived not with light but with a presence.

Kaelen woke to the sensation of being watched. His eyes opened to find Rin still curled against him, her face pressed into his chest, her breathing slow and even. But the air in the room had changed—thicker, heavier, charged with a silence that had nothing to do with dawn.

He sat up slowly, careful not to wake her. The collar around his neck was cold against his skin, the limiter pulsing in time with his heartbeat. The seal beneath his uniform was quiet, but he could feel it listening, as if something had entered its range.

And then he saw her.

Lady Morwen stood in the open doorway.

She was small—shorter than Rin, shorter than Elara—with the compact build of a creature built for speed rather than strength. Her hair was white, so white it seemed to glow in the dim light, pulled back from a face that was ageless and sharp. Her eyes were the color of old blood, and they fixed on Kaelen with an intensity that made the frozen ocean within him crack.

She wore the simple robes of a demon scholar, unadorned, practical. But there was nothing practical about the way she moved when she stepped into the room—a gliding motion that covered ground without sound, without wasted energy, like a blade drawn from a sheath.

“You sleep with a glutton,” she said, her voice soft, almost conversational. “You used to sleep alone. Even your concubines were dismissed before dawn. You said intimacy was a weakness.”

Kaelen kept his expression neutral. “I’ve changed.”
