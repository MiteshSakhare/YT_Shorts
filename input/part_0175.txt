# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
Hands shaking. “You’re asking us to let you walk into a trap. To let you become what they want you to be. To let you…”

“To let me do what I was born to do.” He took her hands, pressing them to his chest. “I spent three hundred years running from what I was. I spent another three months learning to be something new. Now I have to be both. The darkness and the light. The emperor and the king. The two halves of something that was never meant to be whole.”

He leaned forward, his forehead touching hers. “Trust me. The way I’ve learned to trust you.”

She closed her eyes, and for a moment, he felt her tremble. Then she opened them, and they were steady.

“If you don’t come back,” she said quietly, “I will burn that fortress to the ground. I will tear down the Church. I will destroy the darkness that took you. And I will find you, in whatever life comes next, and I will kill you myself.”

He smiled. “Fair.”

Rin was next, her arms wrapping around him, her face buried in his chest. “You taste like fear,” she whispered. “And hope. And something I’ve never tasted before.”

“Love.” She pulled back, her orange eyes bright with tears she refused to shed. “You taste like love. And if you die, I’ll never taste anything again.”

He kissed her forehead. “Then I won’t die.”

Elara was last. She didn’t embrace him. She simply took his hand, pressing something into his palm—a small notebook, its pages blank.
