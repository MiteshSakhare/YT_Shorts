# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
Vex’ahlia was silent for a long moment. “The prophecy says the twice-crowned king will either remake the world or unmake it. I think we’re about to find out which.”

He didn’t sleep that night.

Instead, he sat in the study, the scroll spread before him, Elara’s notebooks stacked beside it. She had found him an hour after Vex’ahlia left, her arms full of books, her dark eyes bright with the particular intensity of a scholar who had found a mystery.

“The darkness was split,” she said, not a question. “Half in you, half wandering. And now they’re reunited.”

“That’s what the scroll says.”

She pulled a chair beside him, her knee brushing his. “Then the question is: what happens when the two halves of something that was never meant to be whole come back together?” She opened one of her notebooks, pages covered in her cramped handwriting. “I’ve been researching soul-bonds, demonic pacts, the old magic that the Church tried to bury. There’s a theory that some things are broken for a reason. That the only way to keep them from destroying everything is to keep them apart.”

He looked at her—at the exhaustion in her face, the ink stains on her fingers, the fierce intelligence in her eyes. “You’ve been working on this since the trial.”

“I’ve been working on this since the night you broke the seal.” She reached out, touching the silver ring on his finger. “I knew something was wrong. The power you released that night—it wasn’t just yours. It was something older. Something that had been waiting.”
