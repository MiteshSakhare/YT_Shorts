# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
“You want to study me,” he said slowly. “In exchange for what?”

“Discretion. Assistance. I know the Academy’s archives better than anyone. I know which Church cadets are corrupt, which demons are plotting against Seraphina, which human nobles are funding the Inquisition’s shadow operations.” She reached out and touched the frost on the window, her finger tracing one of the patterns. “Information is power. And you, Demon Emperor, need power.”

The title hung in the air between them. She knew. He didn’t know how, but she knew.

“How long have you suspected?”

“Since the first night you arrived. You don’t move like a sixteen-year-old. You don’t breathe like one. You don’t sleep like one.” Her finger stopped on a particularly intricate spiral. “And then Rin marked you, and Seraphina claimed you, and the Church came for you, and you stood in that corridor like a man who’s seen a hundred armies and found them wanting.” She looked at him, her eyes bright with something that might have been awe or terror or both. “You’re not a student. You’re a relic. And I want to be close enough to watch what happens when you wake up.”

Kaelen laughed softly. “You’re either very brave or very foolish.”

“I prefer ‘informed.’” She pulled her hand back from the frost, and for a moment, her fingers brushed his knee—a fleeting touch, accidental or deliberate, he couldn’t tell. “I won’t tell anyone. Not the Church, not the demons, not the humans. I’ll help you. I’ll keep your secrets. But when you become what you’re meant to be…” She leaned closer, her breath warm against his ear. “…don’t forget who saw you first.”
