# Part 16: The First Stone

NARRATOR:
"Until one side was strong enough to start again." Elara looked at Kaelen. "That's why they sent Valerius to the Academy. Not to maintain the truce. To break it. To create an excuse for this." She gestured to the army on the horizon. "They were always coming. The Academy was just the pretext."

Kaelen stared at the advancing banners, the pieces clicking into place. Valerius's aggression. The tribunal's haste. The Church's divided loyalties. It had never been about him. He was just the spark they needed to light the fire.

"They wanted a heretic," he said slowly. "They wanted a monster. Something that would justify the war they were already planning."

"And you gave them one," Rin said. Then she grinned. "But you also gave them something they didn't expect. A heretic who doesn't want to burn the world. A monster who chooses to be something else."

Kaelen turned from the window. "How many of the students will fight?"

Seraphina's jaw tightened. "The demon students will fight. They have nowhere else to go. The Church students—Mara's faction—they'll defend the Academy. The humans…" She hesitated. "The humans will have to choose."

"Then we give them a choice." Kaelen walked to the stairwell, his voice carrying. "We open the gates. Anyone who wants to leave can leave. No questions. No judgment. Anyone who wants to stay and fight, we train them. Anyone who wants to stand against us…" He paused at the top of the stairs. "We give them every chance to walk away before we make them regret staying."

The next three days were a blur of preparation.
