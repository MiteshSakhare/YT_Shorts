# Part 21: The Trial of Fire

NARRATOR:
He turned to the delegates. “Valerius was wrong. He committed atrocities. He nearly started a war. But he was also a man who was used—by his own fear, by the darkness in that sword, by a system that taught him to see enemies instead of people. If we execute him, we prove him right. We prove that this council is just another court of vengeance. But if we give him a chance—a real chance—to face what he’s done, to be judged by the people he hurt, to live with the consequences… then we prove that we are something new.”

Lady Ashford’s voice was sharp. “You ask us to show mercy to a murderer?”

“I ask you to show justice. Not the justice of the sword. The justice of truth.” He met her eyes. “Let his victims speak. Let them decide his fate. And let the world see that this council is not afraid to be merciful, even to its enemies.”

The chamber was silent. Then, slowly, Mara rose. “The Church agrees to the proposal. Let the victims decide.”

Lord Malachar nodded. “The demon lords agree.”

All eyes turned to Lady Ashford. Her face was pale, her hands clenched on the table. For a moment, Kaelen thought she would refuse. Then her shoulders sagged.

“The human delegation agrees,” she said quietly. “Let the victims speak.”

Valerius stared at Kaelen, his eyes bright with something that might have been tears. “Why?” he whispered. “Why are you doing this?”
