# Part 12: The Gathering Storm

NARRATOR:
One held by the demon lords. One held by Seraphina. And one held by me.” She pressed the key into his palm, folding his fingers around it. “I’m giving you mine. If the Church comes for you, if Valerius tries to use the limiter against you, you can remove it. But only once. After that, the other two keys will lock it permanently.”

He looked at the key—small, unassuming, a piece of metal that could free him or doom him. “Why?”

“Because I want to see what you become when you’re not caged.” She stepped back, her expression unreadable. “And because I’ve spent too long standing on the wrong side of history. If you’re going to be the storm, I want to be in the center of it, not swept away.”

She turned back to the window, her back to him. “Now go. You have allies to warn. A war to prepare for. And a choice to make.”

He closed his hand around the key, the metal warm against his palm. He wanted to ask more—about the prophecy, about her past, about the woman behind the mask. But he knew she had given him all she was willing to give.

He turned and walked to the door.

“The glutton. The scholar. Seraphina.” Her voice was soft, almost gentle. “They chose you. Not because of what you were, but because of what they see in you. Don’t let them be wrong.”

He left without answering, the key burning a hole in his pocket.
