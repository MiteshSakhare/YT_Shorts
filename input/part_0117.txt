# Part 16: The First Stone

NARRATOR:
Kaelen moved through the Academy like a general preparing for siege, his orders crisp, his presence a steadying force in the chaos. Seraphina organized the demon defenders, her bloodline's authority giving structure to the scattered forces. Rin trained the glutton demons—a dozen of them, each with her hunger, her speed, her ferocity—turning them into a strike force that could move through shadows like blades through silk.

And Elara became the Academy's memory, its historian, its conscience. She catalogued every resource, every exit, every weakness. She found the old wards that had protected the Academy before the truce and taught the students how to activate them. She became, in the space of three days, something none of them had expected: a leader.

On the third night, Kaelen found her in the library, surrounded by scrolls, her hands stained with ink, her eyes red from reading.

"You need to sleep."

"I need to find a way to win." She didn't look up. "The army has siege engines. Catapults, battering rams, something called a 'sun-caster' that the Church blessed. They can level the Academy walls in a day if we let them."

"Then we don't let them." He sat across from her, pulling a scroll toward him. "Rin's gluttons can sabotage the siege lines. Seraphina's demons can hold the walls. And I…"

He stopped. Elara looked up, her eyes sharp. "And you?"

He met her gaze. "I can make them question why they're here. The human soldiers—they're not fighting because they hate demons. They're fighting because their king told them to. Because they believe they're defending their homes, their families, their way of life. If I can make them see the truth—that this war was always about power, not protection—then maybe they'll stop."
