# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
“I would refuse to be a figurehead. If you want a king who sits on a throne and gives orders, find someone else. If you want someone who will help you build something new—something that doesn’t need kings and thrones—then I’ll listen.”

The demon lords exchanged glances. One of them—a woman with moth-like wings and eyes like molten gold—leaned forward. “And what would you build, boy?”

Kaelen met her gaze. “A council. Representatives from all three factions. Humans, demons, Church. No one faction rules. No one faction is above the others. The Academy becomes a neutral ground—a place for negotiation, for education, for building trust. The truce becomes something real, not just a pause between wars.”

“You’re asking us to give up power,” Malachar said.

“I’m asking you to share it. The old way—the way of kings and conquest—it’s over. The army that came here yesterday proved that. Ten thousand soldiers, ready to die for a lie. How many more have to die before we learn?”

Silence. Then the moth-winged woman laughed—a soft, musical sound. “He’s young. He’s idealistic. He’s completely insane.” She looked at Malachar. “I like him.”

Malachar’s expression cracked, just slightly. “You would support this? A council?”

“I would support anything that keeps my children from dying in another war.” She looked at Kaelen. “The old ways have given us nothing but corpses. Perhaps it’s time for something new.”

One by one, the demon lords nodded. Not all—some remained stone-faced, their objections clear in the set of their jaws—but enough. Enough to begin.
