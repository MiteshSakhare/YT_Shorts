# Part 16: The First Stone

NARRATOR:
Kaelen said.

"I didn't think I would either." She walked to the battlements, standing beside him. "But I've spent my whole life watching. Watching the Church become what it was meant to destroy. Watching the king use faith as a weapon. Watching the truce rot from the inside." She looked at him. "You made me want to stop watching."

"Then fight." He handed her a sword—plain steel, unadorned, the same as every other defender carried. "Not for the Church. For the people who can't fight for themselves."

She took the sword, weighing it in her hand. "And when this is over? When the army is beaten back? What then?"

"Then we build something new. Something that doesn't need watching."

She smiled—a real smile, warm and fierce. "I'd like that."

The king's herald came at noon, riding a white horse, bearing a banner of truce. He stopped at the gates, his voice carrying across the walls.

"In the name of King Aldric the Bold, Defender of the Faith, Lord of the Southern Realms, I demand the immediate surrender of the Spire Academy. Those who lay down their arms will be granted mercy. Those who resist will be shown none. The heretic known as Kaelen Vane is to be delivered to the king's justice. Failure to comply will result in total annihilation."
