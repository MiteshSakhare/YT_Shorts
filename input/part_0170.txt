# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
Hers. “And you stayed.”

“I stayed.” She squeezed his fingers. “Because you’re not the darkness. You’re the man who walked into an army and stopped a war. You’re the man who showed mercy to his enemy. You’re the man who holds the darkness and chooses, every day, not to let it win.”

Her voice cracked on the last words, and he saw the fear she’d been hiding—the terror that the man she loved was slowly being consumed by something he couldn’t control.

He pulled her into his lap, her arms wrapping around his neck, her face buried in his shoulder. “I’m not going anywhere.”

“You don’t know that.”

“I know that I have people who won’t let me fall.” He stroked her hair, feeling her tremble. “I know that I have you. Seraphina. Rin. A council that’s learning to trust. A world that’s finally learning to be something other than a battlefield.”

She pulled back, her eyes red-rimmed. “That’s a lot of weight to put on a few people.”

“Then we carry it together.”

She kissed him then, soft and desperate, and he held her, the darkness in his chest quieting, the silver ring warm against her skin.

When she pulled back, her cheeks were flushed, her composure returning. “I’m going to find an answer. There has to be a way to contain the darkness, to separate it from you without destroying you. I’ll find it.”

“I know you will.”

She smiled, small and fierce. “Good. Now get some sleep. You look like death.”

“I’m a scholar. Accuracy is more important than romance.”
