# Part 24: The Twice-Crowned King

NARRATOR:
One year after the fall of Blackthorn, the Academy held a celebration.

It was not a festival of victory. There were no banners proclaiming the defeat of enemies, no speeches about the glory of battle. It was a festival of building—the first stone of the new library laid beside the first stone of the new dormitories, the old walls torn down to make room for gardens, the children of humans and demons and Church acolytes planting trees in soil that had been ash for a hundred years.

Kaelen stood at the edge of the celebration, watching the crowd. Seraphina was beside him, her hand in his, her silver-blue hair loose in the spring wind. Rin was somewhere in the crowd, probably stealing food from the tables, her laughter a bright thread in the chaos. Elara was at the library site, her notebooks open, directing the placement of the foundation stones with the same intensity she had once brought to the study of ancient texts.

“They’re happy,” Seraphina said quietly.

“They’re building something.” He squeezed her hand. “That’s better than happy.”

She leaned into him, her head on his shoulder. “What about us? What are we building?”

He was quiet for a moment, watching the sun set over the Academy walls, the lights of the neutral quarter flickering to life like earthbound stars. “I don’t know. Something that lasts.”

She turned to face him, her golden eyes soft. “You promised my grandmother an heir. A king. A future.”

“I made a lot of promises I didn’t keep.”

“You kept this one.” She touched the silver ring on his finger, the ring that had once been a chain, that had become a promise. “Not the way she expected. But you kept it.”
