# Part 7: The Weight of a Crown

NARRATOR:
Writing was ancient, the ink faded, but the words were clear:

When the twice-crowned king returns,

The bonds of blood shall break and burn.

Three factions fall, one world shall rise,

And truth shall wear a heretic’s eyes.

He looked up. “You think this is about me.”

“I think the timing is… suggestive.” Vex’ahlia’s expression was unreadable. “The prophecy appeared in the Church’s most sacred vaults three months ago. No one knows how it got there. No one knows who wrote it. But every faction has been searching for the ‘twice-crowned king’ ever since. The demon lords think it’s one of their own. The human king thinks it’s a weapon to be controlled. And the Church…” She smiled, and this time there was something almost gentle in her expression. “The Church wants to burn it before it can burn us.”

“I want to see which way it tips.”

She stood, walking around the desk until she was behind him. Her fingers brushed his shoulders, light as feathers. “I’m not going to stop Valerius’s tribunal tonight. I want to see how you handle pressure. How you maneuver. How you fight.” She leaned down, her lips beside his ear. “But when the time comes, when the game is truly on the board… I’ll be watching. And if you prove to be the king the prophecy speaks of…” Her hand slid down his arm, her fingers intertwining with his. “…I’ll be at your side.”

She pulled back, and the warmth of her touch vanished. “Now go. You have a trial to prepare for.”

Kaelen stood, his mind a storm of calculations. He walked to the door, then paused.
