Chaos erupted. Demon students surged forward, human students trying to hold them back, Church cadets drawing weapons. In seconds, the training ground would become a battlefield.

Kaelen looked at Seraphina. Her face was cold, calculating, already assessing the political fallout.

He looked at Gaius, who was trying to restore order, his voice lost in the noise.

He looked at the demon girl pinned against the wall, her eyes wide with terror.

And he made a decision.

He stepped into the center of the chaos, raised his hand, and pushed.

The seal screamed in protest, but he didn’t care. He drew on the frozen ocean beneath the ice, pulling a fraction of his true power into the open. Black light erupted from his palm, not in a wave of destruction but in a pressure—an invisible weight that slammed into every student, every instructor, every combatant, forcing them to their knees.

Silence fell. The only sound was the crackling of his power, the frost spreading across the arena floor.

He looked at the stunned faces around him—human, demon, Church alike—and smiled. Not the meek smile of Kaelen Vane. Not the cold smile of the Demon Emperor.

Something new. Something that belonged to this world, this life, this moment.

“The truce,” he said, his voice carrying to every corner of the arena, “is not broken. A student made a mistake. Another student escalated. It ends here.” He lowered his hand, the pressure receding. “If anyone wants to fight, fight me. I’m not going anywhere.”

The silence stretched. Then Seraphina laughed—a bright, genuine sound that cut through the tension.
