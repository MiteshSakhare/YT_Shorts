# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
Retreated, Kaelen woke to silence.

It was a strange thing—the absence of drums, of shouted orders, of the ever-present tension that had coiled in his chest for weeks. The Academy was still, the kind of stillness that comes after a storm, when the world is catching its breath.

He was in Seraphina’s chambers. He didn’t remember falling asleep, only the long night before—the celebrations that had spilled into the courtyard, the laughter and tears, the way Seraphina had pulled him away from the crowd, her hand tight in his, her eyes saying things she couldn’t speak aloud.

Now she was curled against him, her silver-blue hair spread across his chest, her breathing slow and even. In sleep, she looked younger, the hard lines of command softened, the golden eyes hidden. He traced the curve of her horn with his fingertip, marveling at how something so sharp could feel so warm.

“If you keep doing that,” she murmured, not opening her eyes, “I’ll think you actually like me.”

She smiled, her lips brushing his skin. “Careful. I might start to believe it.”

She shifted, rising above him, her hair falling around their faces like a curtain. Her eyes were open now, gold and bright, searching his face with an intensity that made the air between them feel charged.

“What happens now?” she asked.

“Now we rebuild. The Academy, the truce, the factions. Everything that was broken.”

“And us?” Her hand pressed against his chest, over the place where the seal had been. The skin was smooth now, unmarked, but she touched it like she could still feel the chains.
