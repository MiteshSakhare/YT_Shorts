# Part 12: The Gathering Storm

NARRATOR:
But the peace was fragile.

Three days after the evaluation, a summons arrived. Not from the tribunal, not from the demon lords, but from Vex’ahlia. The message was delivered by a Church acolyte in silver robes, her face impassive, her hands folded.

“Commander Vex’ahlia requests your presence in her chambers. Tonight. Alone.”

Rin, who had been sprawled across Kaelen’s bunk, sat up with a scowl. “Alone? That’s a trap.”

“Everything is a trap,” Kaelen said, taking the message. “The question is who set it.”

Vex’ahlia’s chambers were not what he expected.

The Church quarter was all white stone and silver light, its corridors lined with statues of the Celestial Seers, its air thick with incense. But Vex’ahlia’s private room was sparse, almost ascetic: a bed, a desk, a single window that looked out over the borderlands where human and demon territories met. No icons. No relics. No visible symbols of faith.

She was waiting by the window, her bare feet on the cold stone, her silver hair loose. She wore the same gray tunic as before, unadorned, and when she turned to face him, her tarnished-gold eyes were tired in a way that had nothing to do with physical exhaustion.

“You came,” she said.

“I did.” She gestured to a chair, but he didn’t sit. She didn’t seem to mind. “I’ve been watching you, Kaelen Vane. Since the moment you stepped into this Academy. I watched you play the fool. I watched you reveal yourself. I watched you bind yourself to three women who want very different things from you.” She tilted her head. “Do you know what I see?”
