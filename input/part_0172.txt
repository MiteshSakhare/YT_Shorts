# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
“They destroy the world.”

“Or they make something new.” She leaned forward, her forehead touching his. “You’re not just carrying the darkness, Kaelen. You’re becoming it. And it’s becoming you. The question is what you choose to be when it’s done.”

He reached up, cupping her face in his hands. “And what do you think I’ll choose?”

She smiled, but it was tremulous, uncertain. “I think you’ll choose what you’ve always chosen. The people you love. The world you’re building. The future you’re fighting for.” She kissed him, soft and quick. “I just hope that’s enough.”

She pulled away, climbing off the bed, her energy returning. “Now get dressed. Seraphina’s called a meeting of the council. Something about the borderlands. Apparently, Valerius’s followers haven’t scattered. They’ve been gathering.”

Kaelen was on his feet in an instant. “Where?”

“The old fortress at Blackthorn. A day’s march from the Academy. They’ve got weapons, supplies, and someone leading them.” She tossed him his coat. “Someone who isn’t Valerius.”

He caught the coat, the silver ring blazing on his finger, the darkness in his chest surging. “Who?”

“They’re calling her the Oracle. She appeared after Valerius fell. Claiming to speak for the darkness, for the true faith, for the war that’s supposed to remake the world.” Rin’s face was grim. “She’s the one who gave Valerius the sword.”

The council convened in emergency session, the delegates pale, the guards at the doors doubled.

Kaelen stood at the center of the chamber, Seraphina beside him, Rin at his back, Elara with her notebooks. Across the table, Lady Ashford’s face was ashen.
