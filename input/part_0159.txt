# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
He was quiet for a long moment. “I was. When I first woke in this body, I was terrified of what I’d been. Of what I could become again.” He looked at her. “But I’ve learned that fear isn’t the enemy. It’s what you do with it. I was the Demon Emperor because I let my fear turn into hatred, into hunger, into conquest. I’m not going to let that happen again.”

Vex’ahlia studied him, her expression softening. “You’ve changed more than you know.”

“Or less.” He smiled, small and tired. “We’ll find out.”

She reached out and touched his hand, the one with the silver ring. “You’re not alone in this. Whatever comes, whatever the darkness brings, you have people who will stand with you. People who chose you, not because of what you were, but because of what you’re becoming.”

He looked at her hand on his, at the ring that pulsed with a light that was not his own. “Does that include you?”

She smiled—a real smile, warm and surprising. “I told you before. I want to see what you become. And I’m not going to miss it.”

She squeezed his hand once, then released it, stepping back into the shadows. “Get some sleep, Kaelen. Tomorrow, the real work begins.”

She was gone before he could answer, leaving him alone on the walls with the moon and the stars and the darkness in his chest.
