# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
He had tucked the notebook into his coat and walked toward the gates, the weight of three women's love pressing against his back like a shield.

Now he stood alone, the gates closed behind him, the army of the human king spread out before him like a sea of steel and gold.

The no-man's-land between the Academy and the camp was a hundred paces of ash and thorn, scarred by a century of waiting. His boots crunched on the dead ground, each step deliberate, each breath measured. He did not hide his power. He let it rise, just enough to make the air shimmer, to make the soldiers on the walls of the camp pause in their preparations, to make them wonder what kind of man walked toward them without fear.

By the time he reached the camp's outer perimeter, a crowd had gathered. Soldiers in the king's colors, their faces young, their hands tight on their weapons. Officers with gold braid and hard eyes. And at the center, a figure on horseback, his armor gilded, his cloak white, his face the face of a man who had spent his life being told he was chosen.

King Aldric the Bold.

He was younger than Kaelen had expected—barely thirty, with sandy hair and a clean-shaven jaw, his blue eyes sharp with the particular arrogance of a man who had never been told no. He looked down at Kaelen from the back of his warhorse, his expression a mask of cold authority.
