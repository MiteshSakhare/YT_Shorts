# Part 4: The Glutton’s Appetite

NARRATOR:
Kaelen’s mask didn’t slip. “I don’t know what you’re talking about. I’m just a mediocre student trying not to embarrass my family.”

Gaius studied him for a long moment, then did something unexpected. He smiled. It was a thin, humorless expression. “The mediocre ones are always the most interesting. The Church believes this Academy is a breeding ground for heresy. I believe they’re right.” He reached into his coat and produced a small silver coin, pressing it into Kaelen’s palm. “If you ever need… protection… from the demon filth that seems so interested in you, flip that coin. I’ll know.”

He walked away without waiting for a response.

Kaelen looked at the coin. It was warm to the touch, inscribed with a celestial sigil that pulsed faintly. A tracking device. A leash, from a third party.

He tucked it into his pocket with a mental sigh. Three factions, three leashes, and I haven’t even unpacked my second pair of trousers.

The dormitory that night was a lesson in the Academy’s social hierarchy.

His two human roommates—a boisterous boy named Cedric from a minor barony, and a quiet, sharp-eyed girl named Elara who had been assigned to the male wing due to overcrowding—were already entrenched in their own dramas. Cedric was regaling anyone who would listen about his “heroic” entrance ceremony. Elara sat on her bunk, reading a thick tome on demonic physiology, her dark curls falling over her face.
