# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
“Have you?” She stopped at the foot of the bed, her red eyes traveling over him—the collar, the seal visible through his open shirt, the way his hand had instinctively moved to cover Rin’s sleeping form. “Or are you simply playing a different role?”

Behind him, Rin stirred. Her eyes snapped open, and in an instant she was on her feet, a dagger appearing in her hand from nowhere, her body positioned between Kaelen and the intruder.

“Who the hell—”

“Rin.” Kaelen’s voice was calm. “Stand down.”

Rin didn’t lower the knife, but she didn’t attack either. Her orange eyes flicked between Kaelen and the old demon, reading the tension in the room.

Lady Morwen smiled—a thin, bloodless expression. “A guard dog. How quaint. She was more loyal than most of your generals, if I recall. Though she never slept in your bed.”

“You’re early,” Kaelen said, rising from the bed. His shirt was half-open, his hair disheveled, but he met her gaze without flinching. “Seraphina said you would arrive today.”

“I arrived last night.” Morwen’s eyes drifted to the collar around his neck, and something flickered in her expression—not pity, not triumph, but a kind of weary recognition. “I wanted to see you before the formal evaluation. Before the masks went up.” She tilted her head. “You look younger than I expected. And smaller. The Demon Emperor filled a room with his presence. You… you look like a boy who borrowed a body that doesn’t quite fit.”
