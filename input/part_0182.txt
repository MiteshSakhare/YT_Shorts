# Part 23: The Oracle’s Truth

NARRATOR:
Quietly. “But you were also right. The darkness was meant to return. The twice-crowned king was meant to rise. But not to end the world. To begin it.”

The Oracle’s face crumpled. The years that had been held at bay by her faith crashed into her all at once, and she fell to her knees, a woman broken by the weight of three centuries of waiting.

“I only wanted to serve,” she whispered. “I only wanted to do what was right.”

“I know.” Kaelen knelt beside her, the darkness quiet now, the silver ring steady on his finger. “And you can still serve. Not the darkness. Not the Unmaking. The world that’s being built. The future that’s waiting.” He took her hands, feeling them tremble. “There’s a place for you in it. If you want it.”

She looked up at him, her winter-sky eyes bright with tears. “After everything I’ve done?”

“After everything we’ve all done.” He helped her to her feet, her weight fragile against him. “The world isn’t built by saints. It’s built by people who were broken and chose to be mended.”

He turned to the followers, who were rising from their knees, their faces uncertain, their hands empty. “The darkness is gone. Not destroyed—remade. The Unmaking isn’t coming. There’s no war, no crusade, no final battle. There’s only the work of building something new.” He spread his hands. “You can be part of that. Or you can go back to your old lives, your old hatreds, your old fears. The choice is yours.”

Which brings us back to... “You were wrong,” he said...
