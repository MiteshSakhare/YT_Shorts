# Part 21: The Trial of Fire

NARRATOR:
Scales rippled. “You are not a member of this council, Lord Vane.”

“I am the one who stopped him. I am the one who took the darkness from his sword. I am the one who chose to bring him here instead of killing him.” He stood, and the silver ring on his finger blazed. “I have the right to speak.”

Mara inclined her masked head. “The council grants the request.”

Kaelen walked down to the center of the chamber, stopping before Valerius. The scarred captain raised his head, his eyes meeting Kaelen’s for the first time since the ritual chamber.

“You want to die,” Kaelen said quietly. “You came here expecting execution. You wanted to be a martyr, a symbol, something for your followers to rally around.” He let the words hang in the air, watching Valerius’s face crack. “I’m not going to give you that.”

Valerius’s jaw tightened. “You don’t have a choice. The council will—”

“The council will do what it decides. But I will stand here and tell them what I saw in you. Not the monster. Not the heretic-hunter. The man who, in his last moment, looked at his hands and asked, ‘What have I done?’”
