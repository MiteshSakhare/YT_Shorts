# Part 2: The Serpent’s Welcome

NARRATOR:
Invisible? The thought amused him. In his past life, he had been anything but. He had crushed three realms under his heel. Playing the ghost would be a novelty.

The journey to the Spire Academy took two days by mana-carriage. The landscape shifted from the cultivated estates of the Duchy to the wild, borderlands where the human territories met the demon-held lands. The Academy itself sat astride that border—a colossal structure of black stone and white marble, fused together in architectural tension. It was a monument to an uneasy peace.

As the carriage rolled through the outer gates, Kaelen felt it: a pulse of power from the seal within him. A low, resonant hum. Something within these walls was calling to the frozen ocean inside his soul.

The courtyard was a sea of new students, a hundred noble houses on display. He noted the cliques instantly: the humans in their gold-trimmed blue uniforms, clustered together with practiced arrogance; the demon students in crimson and black, their horns and tails a proud display, keeping to their own; and a smaller group in white and silver—the Church’s wardens, watching everyone with suspicion.

He stepped out, allowing his meek expression to settle onto his features like a mask. A servant directed him toward the dormitory assignment pavilion. He was halfway there when a voice, smooth as poisoned honey, stopped him.

“The boy who sleeps through his destiny. How quaint.”
