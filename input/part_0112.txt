# Part 15: The Throne of Embers

NARRATOR:
“I don’t know how to do this,” he said quietly. “I don’t know how to be… this. Someone people want. Someone people stay for.”

Seraphina’s hand found his thigh, her touch grounding. “Neither do we. But we’re figuring it out.”

Rin pressed closer, her lips brushing his ear. “Together.”

Elara squeezed his hand. “Together.”

He closed his eyes, and for a moment, the frozen ocean within him was still. Not frozen—still. As if, for the first time, it was at peace.

Later that night, when the others had gone, Kaelen stood at the window of his study, looking out at the borderlands. The moon was full, painting the no-man’s-land between human and demon territories in shades of silver and shadow.

A knock came at the door. Not a request—an announcement.

Vex’ahlia entered, her bare feet silent on the stone. She was dressed as always in gray, her silver hair loose, her tarnished-gold eyes sharp. She looked at the cushions, at the scattered books, at the faint scent of three different women still lingering in the air, and smiled.

“You’ve been busy.”

“I’ve been alive.” He turned to face her. “What do you want?”

She walked to the window, standing beside him. “To tell you that Valerius has fled. He took a squad of loyalists and disappeared into the human territories. He’s going to the king. He’s going to tell him that the Academy has fallen, that the demons have seized control, that you are the herald of a new war.”

Kaelen’s jaw tightened. “How long?”
