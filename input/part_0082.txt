# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
He kissed her.

It was not gentle. It was not the kiss of a boy to a girl. It was the kiss of one ancient soul to another, of two halves of a broken whole finding each other in the dark. The seal erupted between them, black light and silver, the frozen ocean surging against its chains, and Seraphina gasped against his mouth, her hands fisting in his shirt.

When they broke apart, they were both breathing hard, the room colder than before, frost spreading across the window glass.

Seraphina's eyes were bright, her lips parted, her composure shattered. "That," she whispered, "was not what I expected."

"What did you expect?"

"I don't know." She laughed, a shaky, genuine sound. "Something more controlled. Something more… emperor-like."

He touched her face, tracing the line of her jaw. "I'm not the emperor anymore. I don't know what I am. But I'm tired of pretending I don't want the people who want me."

She leaned into his touch, her eyes closing. "All of them? The glutton? The scholar?"

"All of you." He didn't know when he'd made the decision—perhaps on the rooftop with Rin, perhaps in the library with Elara, perhaps in this room with Seraphina. Perhaps it had been made for him, by the seal that bound them all together. "I don't know if it's the pact. I don't know if it's the bond. But I know I don't want to be alone anymore. And you—all of you—make me not alone."
