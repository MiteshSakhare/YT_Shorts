# Part 4: The Glutton’s Appetite

NARRATOR:
Seraphina tilted her head, a small smile playing on her lips. “The Vane line is the Valtiel line. Your grandmother was my father’s sister. She married into the Vane duchy to cement an alliance. When your parents died, you were sent back to us. Or rather, you were sent to the Duke.” She leaned forward, resting her chin on her hands. “But the soul that returned was not the sickly little boy I remembered. You moved differently. Spoke differently. And when I touched you at the pavilion…” Her smile sharpened. “I felt him. The Demon Emperor. The man who made a vow to my ancestor three hundred years ago and then abandoned her to rot in a seal of his own making.”

Kaelen’s mind raced. Her ancestor. The ancient voice from within the seal. “You speak as if you know me. But I have no memory of any vow.”

“Of course you don’t.” Seraphina’s voice dripped with scorn. “You were dying when you made it. Your empire was crumbling, your generals betraying you. You came to my great-grandmother—the most powerful pact-maker of our age—and swore an oath. In exchange for salvation, you would return to her when she called. You would give her what was promised.”

“And what was promised?”

Seraphina stood, circling the table until she was behind him. Her fingers brushed his shoulders, trailing down his arms. “Everything. Your power. Your soul. Your self.” She leaned down, her lips beside his ear. “You belong to the Valtiel bloodline, Demon Emperor. You always have. The seal on your power is my inheritance. I can open it—or I can leave it frozen forever, a weak, pathetic noble boy playing at being a man.”
