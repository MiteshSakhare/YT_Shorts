# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
He pulled on his shirt, his fingers finding the buttons with practiced ease. “Then I’ll have to give her a reason to change her mind.”

“By being the Demon Emperor she remembers? Or by being someone else?”

He didn’t answer. He didn’t know how.

The evaluation was held in the same chamber where the tribunal had convened, but the seating was different. The tiered galleries were empty—this was not a spectacle for students. Instead, a small group of observers sat in a semicircle around the central platform: Seraphina, her expression unreadable; Vex’ahlia, her tarnished-gold eyes watching with the patience of a spider; three demon lords in formal robes, their faces ancient and cold; and a single human representative, a woman with the insignia of the king’s own court.

Lady Morwen stood at the center of the platform, her small frame somehow dominating the space. Beside her, a crystal obelisk—larger than the one in the Mana Weaving class, its facets dark and waiting.

Kaelen walked onto the platform, the collar heavy around his neck, the seal pulsing in counterpoint to his heartbeat. He stopped before the obelisk and waited.

“The evaluation is simple,” Morwen said, her voice carrying to every corner of the chamber. “Place your hand on the obelisk. Allow your mana to flow freely. The crystal will record your power, your affinity, your nature. There will be no deception. No masking. No holding back.”

She met his eyes. “This is not the tribunal. I am not here to judge your actions. I am here to judge what you are. And when the crystal speaks, everyone in this room will know the truth.”

Kaelen looked at the obelisk. Its dark facets seemed to drink the light, waiting, hungry.
