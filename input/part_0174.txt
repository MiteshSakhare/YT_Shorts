# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
Seraphina’s hand found his, her grip iron. “Then we stop her.”

“How?” Lady Ashford’s voice was sharp. “We have no army. The king won’t march again. The demon lords are stretched thin. And she has the faithful—people who believe she speaks for God, for the darkness, for the end of the world. You can’t fight faith with swords.”

Kaelen was quiet for a long moment. Then he looked at the delegates, at Seraphina, at Rin, at Elara, at Vex’ahlia.

“Then I go alone.”

The words hit the chamber like a thunderclap. Seraphina’s grip tightened painfully. Rin made a sound like a wounded animal. Even Vex’ahlia’s composure cracked.

“No,” Seraphina said, her voice low and dangerous. “No. You are not walking into another army alone.”

“This isn’t an army,” Kaelen said. “It’s a congregation. They don’t want to fight. They want to believe. And the only thing they believe more than her is the darkness she claims to speak for.” He touched his chest. “I have that darkness. I can show them what it really is. What it can become when it’s not used as a weapon.”

Elara stepped forward, her face pale. “You’re talking about walking into a fortress full of fanatics and showing them the very thing they worship.”

“I’m talking about giving them a choice. The same choice I was given. The same choice Valerius was given.” He met her eyes. “If it works, we end this without a war. If it doesn’t…” He looked at Seraphina. “If it doesn’t, you’ll know what I became. And you’ll do what needs to be done.”
