# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
Aldric's hand tightened on his sword. For a moment, Kaelen saw the war in his eyes—the pride, the fear, the desperate need to prove that he was not his father, not his grandfather, that he was the Lion his people believed him to be.

Then the king drew his sword.

"I am Aldric the Bold," he said, his voice shaking but steady. "I do not retreat. I do not surrender. I do not bow to demons and heretics." He raised his blade, its edge catching the pale light of dawn. "If you want to stop this war, heretic, you will have to kill me."

Kaelen looked at the sword, at the king, at the ten thousand soldiers watching with held breath. He thought of the Demon Emperor he had been, the man who would have answered this challenge with fire and death, who would have painted the no-man's-land red and called it justice.

He closed his eyes.

When he opened them, the power within him rose—not in anger, not in destruction, but in something else. Something that had no name, no precedent, no place in any prophecy or any war.

He reached out and touched the king's sword.

The blade shattered.

Not with force, not with violence. It simply broke, the steel crumbling to dust in Aldric's hand, the fragments falling through his fingers like sand. The king stared at his empty fist, his face draining of color, his bravado crumbling with his blade.

Kaelen stood before him, empty-handed, and spoke.
