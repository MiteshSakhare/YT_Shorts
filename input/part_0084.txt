# Part 12: The Gathering Storm

NARRATOR:
“Tell me about your empire,” she said one evening, her head on his chest, her silver-blue hair spilling across his arm.

“The beginning. Before you were the Demon Emperor. When you were just… someone who wanted more.”

He was quiet for a long moment. The memories were old, worn smooth by time, but they still had edges. “I was born in a city that no longer exists. My father was a blacksmith. My mother died when I was young. I was nothing. No name, no power, no future.” His voice was flat, the words stripped of emotion. “I wanted more. So I took it. One city. One kingdom. One realm. I kept taking until there was nothing left to take.”

Seraphina’s fingers traced the seal on his chest, the scar beneath his shirt. “And when you had everything?”

“I realized I had nothing.” He looked at the ceiling, at the shadows cast by the candlelight. “An empire of ashes. Generals who feared me, subjects who hated me, allies who waited for the moment to strike. I built a mountain of corpses and sat on top of it, alone.”

She was silent for a moment, then shifted, rising above him, her hair falling like a curtain around their faces. “You’re not alone now.”

He reached up, brushing a strand of hair from her cheek. “No.”

She kissed him, soft and slow, and the seal pulsed between them—not a chain, but a current, a connection that hummed with something that might have been peace.
