# Part 23: The Oracle’s Truth

NARRATOR:
Through the flood, he saw her. Not the Oracle. Not the darkness. A face he had seen in his dreams, in his memories, in the moments between waking and sleeping.

Not the Seraphina who stood beside him in the Academy. The Seraphina who had been waiting for three hundred years. The Seraphina whose bloodline had bound him, whose love had saved him, whose face was the last thing he saw before the darkness took him.

“You promised me,” she whispered. “You promised me a king. Not a conqueror. Not a destroyer. A king who would build something that lasts.”

He thought of the Academy, of the students from all three factions sharing meals, sharing laughter, sharing a future. He thought of Rin’s hunger finally satisfied, Elara’s questions finally answered, Seraphina’s waiting finally ended. He thought of the council, fragile and new, learning to trust. He thought of the world they were building, stone by stone, choice by choice.

And he chose.

He reached into the flood, into the darkness that was consuming him, and he pulled. Not to contain it. Not to destroy it. To shape it.

The black light twisted, writhed, screamed. The Oracle’s face went white. “No! You can’t—you have to let it go! You have to become what you were meant to be!”

“I am.” Kaelen’s voice was calm, steady, the voice of a man who had finally stopped running. “You said the darkness was meant to unmake the world. But you were wrong. It was meant to remake it. And so was I.”
