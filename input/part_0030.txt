# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
She pulled back and climbed back into her bunk without another word, the blanket swallowing her small form.

Kaelen sat motionless, the frost on the window slowly melting. His knee still tingled where her fingers had touched.

Three factions, he thought again. Three demonesses—no, two demonesses and one human scholar. And now a fourth player who wants to be my chronicler.

He looked at Elara’s bunk. She was already pretending to sleep, her breathing deliberately even.

Or my accomplice. Or my confessor. Or…

He pushed the thought aside. He had survived three centuries as the Demon Emperor by trusting no one and using everyone. That wouldn’t change now.

But as he finally lay down, staring at the ceiling, he couldn’t shake the feeling that the game was becoming more complicated than he’d anticipated. Seraphina wanted his power and his bloodline. Rin wanted his taste. Gaius wanted to see which way he tipped. And now Elara wanted to watch him become something terrible, as if it were a privilege.

They’re all collecting me, he realized. Each of them wants a piece of what I was. None of them know what I’m becoming.

He closed his eyes and, for the first time in this new life, allowed himself to dream.

He dreamed of a woman in white robes, her face obscured, her voice ancient and furious. He dreamed of a vow spoken in a language he no longer understood. He dreamed of chains of light wrapping around a heart of darkness, squeezing, binding, owning.

And in the dream, a hand reached through the chains—a woman’s hand, slender and warm—and touched his face.
