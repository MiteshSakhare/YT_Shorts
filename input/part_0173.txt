# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
Voice tight. “We have reports from a dozen villages. She’s been gathering Valerius’s followers, promising them a new crusade. She says the heretic who stopped the king’s army is a false prophet, that the true darkness is coming, that only by embracing it can the world be saved.”

Lord Malachar’s scales rippled. “And what is this darkness she speaks of?”

“She doesn’t name it,” Mara said, her masked face turned toward Kaelen. “But her followers do. They call it the Unmaking. The end of all things. The final war that will burn away the old world and leave only the faithful.”

Kaelen’s hand went to his chest, where the darkness pulsed. “She’s talking about what’s inside me.”

The chamber went silent. Lady Ashford’s eyes widened. “You know this woman?”

“I know what she wants.” He looked at the delegates, at the fear in their faces. “The darkness in me was split three hundred years ago. Half in the seal, half wandering. The wandering half found Valerius. Now it’s found someone else. Someone who knows what it is. Someone who wants to set it free.”

Vex’ahlia stepped out of the shadows. “The Oracle. I’ve heard the name before. She was a priestess in the Church, before the truce. She was the one who first recorded the prophecy of the twice-crowned king. She disappeared the same night Valerius fled the Academy.”

Kaelen’s blood went cold. “She’s been planning this from the beginning. Valerius was a tool. The sword was a way to draw the darkness out. And now…” He looked at the map of the borderlands, at the fortress at Blackthorn marked in red. “Now she’s going to use it to start the war that Valerius couldn’t.”
