The fortress at Blackthorn was a wound in the landscape, its black stone walls rising from the ash and thorn like a scar. Once it had been a Church outpost, a bastion against the demon lands. Now it was a shrine to something older, something that had been waiting in the dark for three hundred years.

Kaelen walked alone.

The silver ring on his finger pulsed with every step, the darkness in his chest a slow, steady thunder. He had left the Academy at dawn, crossing the borderlands with the speed of a man who had nothing left to fear. The followers of the Oracle had seen him coming—he had made no effort to hide—and now they lined the walls, their faces masks of fervor and fear.
