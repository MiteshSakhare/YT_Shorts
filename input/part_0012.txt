# Part 3: The Pawn’s First Move

NARRATOR:
Her lips. “Consider it a down payment. I’m Rin. And I’ve decided you’re going to be mine.”

She vanished into the crowd, leaving Kaelen leaning against the wall, his hand pressed to the small wound on his neck. The seal within him had pulsed at her touch—not in warning, but in recognition.

Another player, he thought, a cold smile finally breaking through his mask. And she’s already marked me as her territory.

He straightened his uniform, noting with grim amusement that his first day had ended with not one but two demonesses staking claims on him. In his old life, he would have crushed such presumption instantly.

But in this life, he was weak. And weakness, he knew, was the best camouflage for a predator learning new hunting grounds.

He touched the mark on his neck. It was already healing, but he could still feel Rin’s mana humming beneath his skin, a second leash alongside Seraphina’s.

Two leashes, he thought, walking toward the healers with a new purpose. Time to learn which one breaks first.
