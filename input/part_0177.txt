# Part 23: The Oracle’s Truth

NARRATOR:
Looking up at the battlements. Men and women in the remnants of Church armor, their eyes bright with a faith that had curdled into something fanatical. They held swords, torches, banners painted with the symbol of the Unmaking—a circle split in two, light and darkness, eternally divided.

“I’m here to see the Oracle,” he called, his voice carrying across the courtyard.

The gates opened.

He walked into the fortress, the crowd parting before him like water before a stone. They whispered as he passed—the heretic, the twice-crowned, the vessel—their voices a susurrus of fear and longing. He felt the darkness in his chest stir in response, reaching toward them, toward the hunger that lived in their eyes.

He pushed it down.

The inner courtyard was empty except for a single figure, standing at the center of a circle of black stone. She was small, smaller than he had expected, with silver hair that fell past her waist and eyes the color of winter sky. She wore the robes of a Church priestess, but they had been altered, the white stained gray, the symbols of the Celestial Seers replaced with the broken circle of the Unmaking.

She smiled when she saw him. “Kaelen Vane. Or should I say, Demon Emperor. Or perhaps, twice-crowned king.” Her voice was soft, almost gentle, the voice of a woman who had spent a lifetime learning to make the terrible sound reasonable. “I’ve waited so long to meet you.”

He stopped a few feet away, close enough to see the lines of age on her face, the weariness in her eyes. “You’re the one who gave Valerius the sword.”
