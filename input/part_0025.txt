Seraphina turned to Kaelen, her expression unreadable. “You have a talent for attracting chaos. That’s useful. But it also makes you a target. From now on, you don’t go anywhere without one of my people. Not the mess hall. Not the library. Not the latrine.”

“And if I refuse?”

She stepped closer, close enough that he could smell the roses in her hair, feel the warmth radiating from her skin. “Then the next time the Church comes for you, I might not be fast enough to pull them back. You’re valuable to me, Emperor. But you’re not indispensable.” Her hand came up to touch his chin, tilting his face toward hers. “Prove your worth, and I’ll give you everything. Fail me, and I’ll let them have you. And then I’ll watch as the seal eats the last of your power and leaves you a hollow shell, begging for a death that won’t come.”

Her thumb brushed his lower lip, a gesture so intimate and threatening that Kaelen felt the seal pulse in response.

“Do we understand each other?”

He held her gaze. “Perfectly.”

She smiled—a genuine smile, warm and terrifying. “Good. Now get some rest. Tomorrow, we begin.”
