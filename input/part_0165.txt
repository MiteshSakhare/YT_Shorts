# Part 21: The Trial of Fire

NARRATOR:
“No.” She looked up at him, her golden eyes soft. “You’re someone I never expected. Someone I’m proud to stand beside.”

He kissed her forehead, and they waited.

The council’s verdict came at sunset.

Valerius was brought back to the center of the chamber, his chains removed. He stood alone, his hands free, his face turned toward the delegates.

Lady Ashford rose. “The council has deliberated. We have heard the victims. We have weighed the crimes against the circumstances. And we have reached a decision.”

She unrolled a scroll, her voice steady. “Valerius of the Celestial Inquisition, you are guilty of incitement to violence, breach of the truce, and complicity in the deaths of twelve Church acolytes and seven demon civilians. These crimes are severe. They cannot be undone.”

Valerius’s face was impassive, but Kaelen saw his hands clench at his sides.

“However,” Lady Ashford continued, “the council recognizes that you were acting under the influence of a corrupting force beyond your control. The darkness in your sword was not of your making, and its influence on your actions was documented by multiple witnesses.”

She lowered the scroll, looking at Valerius with eyes that were not kind, but were not cruel either. “Your sentence is this: you will be stripped of your rank, your titles, and your authority within the Church. You will spend the remainder of your life in service to the Academy—not as a soldier, but as a laborer. You will work alongside the people you wronged. You will see, every day, the consequences of your actions. And if you complete ten years of service without incident, you will be granted a full pardon.”
