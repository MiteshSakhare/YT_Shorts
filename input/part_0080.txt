# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
She looked up, her dark eyes fierce. "I'm not a fighter. I'm not a demon. I can't mark you or claim you or bind you with magic. But I can know things. I can find the truth that everyone else is too afraid to look for. And I can give it to you—all of it—if you let me stay."

Kaelen studied her face—the intelligence in her eyes, the tension in her jaw, the way her hand trembled slightly in his. She was afraid. Not of him, but of his answer.

He didn't give her one. Not with words.

He turned his hand, interlacing their fingers, and squeezed once.

Elara's breath caught. Then she smiled—small, fragile, genuine—and squeezed back.

That night, Seraphina summoned him to her private quarters.

The room was a study in contrasts—demon architecture softened by human luxuries, silk drapes and carved obsidian, candles that burned with golden flame. She was waiting by the window, her silver-blue hair loose, her gown a simple slip of crimson that left her shoulders bare.

She didn't turn when he entered. "You've been busy. The glutton. The scholar. Both of them circling you like moths to a flame."

"I don't control what they choose."

"No." She turned then, her golden eyes glittering in the candlelight. "But you don't discourage them either. That's new."

He walked into the room, stopping a few feet away. "You said you wanted me to choose you. That means letting me choose."

"And have you?" She stepped closer, her bare feet silent on the stone floor. "Chosen?"
