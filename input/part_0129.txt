You, Aldric. I need you to understand." He met the king's eyes, and for a moment, he let Aldric see—not the Demon Emperor, not the heretic, but the man who had spent three hundred years learning that power without purpose was nothing but destruction. "You are not your father. You are not your grandfather. You are a man who was given a crown and told he had to be strong to keep it. But strength isn't conquest. Strength isn't fear. Strength is knowing when to stop."

He stepped back, giving the king room to breathe, to think, to choose. "Call off the attack. Go home. Be the king your people need, not the warrior your pride demands. And when the truce is rebuilt—when the Academy is open again, when the factions have learned to live together—come back. Not as a conqueror. As a partner."

Aldric stared at him, his hand still raised, his empty fingers still trembling. Around them, the army waited, the officers waited, the whole world seemed to hold its breath.

And then, slowly, the king lowered his hand.

"Who are you?" he whispered.

Kaelen smiled—not the cold smile of the Demon Emperor, not the meek smile of the Duke's son, but something new. Something that belonged to this world, this life, this moment.

"I'm someone who learned too late that winning isn't the same as being right," he said. "And I'm trying to be better."

Aldric looked at him for a long, silent moment. Then he looked at his empty hand, at the dust of his sword on his fingers, at the walls of the Academy glowing with light that was neither demonic nor divine.

He turned to his officers. "Stand down."
