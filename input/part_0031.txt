# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
And her voice was Seraphina’s, but older, colder, deeper. “You promised me an heir. You promised me a king. You promised me everything, and then you ran. But you can’t run from a pact, little emperor. You can only pay.”

He woke with a gasp, his hand pressed to his chest. The seal was burning, a brand of heat and light that faded as consciousness returned. His roommates slept on. The window was clear, the frost gone.

But on his pillow, there was a single rose petal—blood-red, impossibly fresh, smelling of Seraphina’s perfume.

He crushed it in his fist, and when he opened his hand, the petal had turned to ash.

She’s in my dreams, he realized, cold fury settling in his bones. She’s in my head. She’s in my blood.

He stood, dressing quickly in his Academy uniform. The sun was rising, casting pale gold light across the dormitory. Today was the first day of formal classes—combat training, where students were paired based on skill and forced to spar in front of the entire student body.

A perfect opportunity for Seraphina to test him. A perfect opportunity for the Church to provoke him. A perfect opportunity for someone to die.

He left the dormitory, and as he walked toward the training grounds, he felt the seal pulse three times—once for Seraphina, once for Rin, once for something else. Something new.
