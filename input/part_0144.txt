# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
He walked to the edge of the circle, where Seraphina was waiting. She took his hand, her fingers cold against his.

“Let them figure it out,” she murmured. “They will, or they won’t. Either way, it’s not yours to carry alone.”

He nodded, and together they walked out of the chamber, leaving the delegates to the slow, painful work of building peace.

The weeks that followed were a lesson in patience.

Kaelen had expected the council to move slowly. He had not expected it to move like a glacier. Lady Ashford demanded reparations for the families of soldiers who had died in the retreat. Lord Malachar insisted on formal recognition of demon territories. Mara pushed for a new charter that would limit the Church’s military authority while preserving its spiritual role.

Each session was a war of words, each agreement a battlefield. But slowly, painfully, something began to take shape.

Kaelen attended the sessions when they needed him—which was often, at first, then less so. He became a presence, not a participant. A reminder of what they were trying to build, and what they would lose if they failed.

In the spaces between, he rebuilt himself.

He walked the Academy’s corridors at dawn, watching the students from all three factions share meals, share classes, share laughter. He trained with Rin in the empty sparring yards, relearning the movements of a body that was finally his own. He read with Elara in the library, her head on his shoulder, her voice soft as she explained the histories he had never known.

And at night, he came home to Seraphina.
