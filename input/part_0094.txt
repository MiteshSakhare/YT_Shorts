# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
They burned crimson, the color of old empires and older hatreds. The white hair at his temples darkened to black, streaking through the silver like smoke through ice. And the seal on his chest—the mark that had bound him for three centuries—shattered, its pieces falling away like shed skin.

For a moment, he was what he had been. The conqueror. The tyrant. The man who had burned three realms and called it glory.

Then he looked at Seraphina, pale against the pillar, her hand still outstretched toward him. He looked at Elara, shielding her face from the cold, her books scattered, her expression not afraid but wondering. He thought of Rin, running through the tunnels to bring him freedom.

And he chose.

He pulled the darkness back. Not suppressing it—that was what the seal had done—but shaping it. The black light dimmed, the frost receded, and when he stood again, he was still Kaelen Vane. The crimson eyes faded to a deep, steady violet. The black in his hair receded to a single streak, a reminder of what lay beneath.

But the power was there. For the first time in this life, he could feel it—not a frozen ocean beneath ice, but a river flowing through him, waiting to be directed.

He knelt beside Seraphina, lifting her gently. “Are you hurt?”

She stared at him, her eyes wide. “Your eyes. They were—”
