# Part 14: The Heretic's Coronation

NARRATOR:
She turned to the assembled factions, her voice rising. "The truce is broken. But something new can rise from its ashes. Kaelen Vane has offered us a choice—not war, not submission, but a chance to build something together. I, for one, am tired of fighting old wars. I choose to build."

She released his hand and stepped back, her place taken by Lord Malachar. The demon lord studied Kaelen with ancient, calculating eyes.

"The Valtiel bloodline has waited three hundred years for what was promised," he said slowly. "A king. An heir. A future. I don't know if you're that king. But Seraphina believes you are. And she has never been wrong about anything that matters."

He inclined his head—not quite a bow, but close. "The demon lords will watch. We will wait. And if you prove worthy, we will follow."

He stepped back, and the human ambassador stepped forward. Her face was pale, her hands trembling, but her voice was steady.

"The human king sent me here to maintain the truce at any cost. I failed. The truce is broken. But perhaps… perhaps something better can be built." She looked at Kaelen, her eyes searching. "You saved our students. You didn't have to. You could have let them burn with the rest. But you didn't."

"I don't know what you are. I don't know what you'll become. But I know you're not the monster they said you were. And for now, that's enough."

One by one, the assembled leaders knelt—demon lords and Church inquisitors, human ambassadors and Academy faculty. The students followed, uncertain but hopeful, their fear giving way to something that might have been faith.
