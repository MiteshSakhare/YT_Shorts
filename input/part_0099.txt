He stepped out of the tunnel and into the chaos.

Valerius saw him first.

The captain's sword was raised, its celestial light casting long shadows across the courtyard. His squad had cornered a group of demon students against the far wall—young, terrified, their horns dull with fear. Valerius was savoring the moment, his scarred face twisted with satisfaction.

Then Kaelen walked into the light, and everything stopped.

He was not the meek Duke's son anymore. The collar was gone, the broken seal visible through his torn shirt, the black streak in his white hair catching the firelight. His eyes—deep violet, but edged with something older, something that had burned realms—fixed on Valerius with the weight of three hundred years.

"Captain," he said, his voice calm, almost conversational. "I believe you were looking for me."

Valerius's sword wavered. For a moment, something flickered in his eyes—not fear, but recognition. The same recognition that came when a lesser predator realized it had been hunting something far larger than itself.

"Heretic," he spat, recovering. "You broke your limiter. You've revealed your true nature. By the authority of the Celestial Inquisition, I place you under arrest."

"By what authority?" Kaelen walked forward, and the Church cadets fell back, their swords rising defensively. "The truce that you're burning to the ground? The laws you swore to uphold? Or the fear that's been eating at you since the moment you first saw me?"
