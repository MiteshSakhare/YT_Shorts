# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
Seraphina, at the defenders gathering in the courtyard, at the sun rising over the Academy walls. “Because if I can save him, then maybe I was worth saving too.”

Rin was quiet for a moment. Then she smiled—her real smile, the one that reached her eyes. “You were worth saving long before that.”

She leaned in, her lips brushing his cheek, her breath warm against his skin. “Don’t forget that.”

He didn’t answer. He didn’t need to. He stood in the courtyard, Seraphina’s hand in his, Rin’s warmth at his side, and watched the sun rise on a world that was still standing.

The council convened an hour later, Valerius brought before them in chains. He was a broken man, his face hollow, his hands trembling, the corruption gone but the memory of it written in every line of his face.

Lady Ashford stared at him with cold fury. “You attacked a Church outpost. You murdered priests. You brought an army to the gates of the Academy. What do you have to say for yourself?”

Valerius raised his head, his eyes finding Kaelen. “I was wrong.”

The words hung in the air, simple and devastating. Lady Ashford’s composure cracked. Even Lord Malachar seemed taken aback.

“I was wrong,” Valerius repeated, his voice shaking. “I believed I was serving the faith. I believed I was protecting the innocent. But I was serving my own fear. My own hatred. And the innocent died because of it.”
