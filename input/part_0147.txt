# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
Kaelen raised a hand, and the chamber fell silent. He had not spoken in the council for weeks, and his voice still carried a weight that none of them could ignore.

“Valerius wants a reaction. He wants us to move against him, to look like we’re suppressing dissent, to give his followers a reason to fight.” He looked at the delegates. “We don’t give him that. We investigate quietly. We secure the border. And we let him make the next move.”

Lady Ashford frowned. “And if he attacks again?”

“Then we respond. But we respond with the council’s authority, not with fear.” He met her eyes. “The people who follow Valerius are scared. They’ve lost their purpose, their faith, their reason to fight. We show them that there’s another way. We show them that the peace we’re building is stronger than the war he wants.”

Lord Malachar nodded slowly. “And if they won’t be shown?”

Kaelen’s jaw tightened. “Then we protect the innocent. And we let Valerius answer for what he’s done. Not in a war. In a courtroom.”

The delegates exchanged glances. Then, one by one, they nodded.

It was not a perfect solution. It was not even a good one. But it was the first decision the council had made unanimously, and as the delegates filed out of the chamber, Kaelen felt something shift in the room. Not trust, exactly. But the beginning of it.

That night, Kaelen found Elara in the library, her notebooks spread across the table, her face pale.

“You heard about the attack,” he said, sitting across from her.
