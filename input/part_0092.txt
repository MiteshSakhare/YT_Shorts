# Part 13: The Unbinding

NARRATOR:
Held. The demon lords’ sigil and Seraphina’s bloodline mark blazed with light, tightening the collar’s grip. He had one hour. One hour to remove the other two keys, or the collar would seal forever, and his power would be bound until the day he died.

“The demon lords’ key,” Seraphina said, her voice tight. “It’s held by Lord Malachar. He’s in the Academy, in the demon quarter. I can—”

“No.” Kaelen shook his head. “If you go to him, he’ll demand something in return. A concession. A promise. I won’t put you in that position.”

“I’ll take it.”

The voice came from the shadows. Rin stepped into the circle of torchlight, her dagger sheathed, her expression grim. “Lord Malachar owes me a debt. Three years ago, I saved his youngest son from a mana collapse. He said he’d repay me anything, any time.” She looked at Kaelen. “I’m calling it in.”

Seraphina’s eyes widened. “Rin, if you do this, you’ll be openly aligned with him. With us. There’s no going back.”

“There’s no going back for any of us.” Rin’s grin was fierce. “I made my choice the first night I tasted him.” She looked at Kaelen, and her voice softened. “I’ll be back before the hour’s up. Don’t do anything stupid while I’m gone.”

She vanished into the tunnels, her footsteps fading into silence.

Elara looked up from her books. “That leaves Seraphina’s key. The bloodline lock. It’s tied to her life force. Only she can remove it.”

Seraphina’s hand tightened on Kaelen’s. “I know.”

“It will hurt,” Elara said quietly. “Breaking a bloodline bond always hurts. And when it’s broken, you won’t be able to remake it. Not ever.”
