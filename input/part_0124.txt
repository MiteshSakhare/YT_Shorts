# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
"So. The heretic walks into my camp alone. Are you surrendering, or simply too foolish to be afraid?"

Kaelen stopped ten paces from the king's horse. "I'm here to ask you a question, Your Majesty."

"A question." Aldric's lips curled. "You presume much, heretic."

"I presume nothing. I'm simply a man who wants to know why ten thousand soldiers are preparing to die for a lie."

The officers around the king bristled. Hands went to sword hilts. Voices rose in anger. But Aldric raised a hand, and silence fell.

"A lie," he repeated, his voice soft. "You claim my war is based on a lie."

"Your war is based on the word of a man who broke the truce he was sworn to uphold." Kaelen's voice was steady, carrying across the camp. "Captain Valerius attacked the demon quarter without provocation. He tortured students. He burned the neutral zones. He created the chaos he claimed to be fighting. And when his crimes were exposed, he fled. He came to you not with the truth, but with the story that would get you to march."

Aldric's face was unreadable. "You expect me to believe the word of a heretic over the word of my own captain?"

"I expect you to ask yourself why Valerius ran. If he was so righteous, if his cause was so just, why did he flee the moment the truth came out? Why did he come to you in secret, without witnesses, without evidence?" Kaelen took a step closer. "Why is he not here now, to face the man he accused?"
