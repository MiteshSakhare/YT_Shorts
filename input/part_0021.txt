# Part 5: The Heretic’s Gambit

NARRATOR:
The corridor outside the demon common hall was chaos.

Students in white and silver uniforms advanced in a disciplined wedge, their leader a tall Church cadet with a scar running from temple to jaw. Behind them, demon students formed a ragged defensive line, arcane energy crackling between their horns and claws. A human girl lay bleeding on the floor—caught in the crossfire, her robes singed.

“By order of the Celestial Inquisition,” the scarred cadet announced, his voice cutting through the din, “hand over the heretic Kaelen Vane. He has been identified as a carrier of corrupt mana—a threat to the truce and to every soul in this Academy. Resist, and we will treat this quarter as hostile territory.”

Kaelen stood in the doorway of the dining chamber, Seraphina a step behind him, Rin flanking his left with a feral grin. Vorg and Lyss had moved to block the entrance.

“Interesting,” Kaelen murmured, loud enough for only Seraphina to hear. “They came here directly. Someone gave them my location.”

“Your Inquisition pet,” Seraphina said coldly. “The blond one. He marked you with that coin, didn’t he?”

Kaelen reached into his pocket and pulled out the silver coin. It was blazing hot, the celestial sigil spinning like a compass needle pointing straight at his chest. “So he did.”

He stepped forward, into the corridor, and the chaos paused. A hundred eyes turned to him—the dull Duke’s son, the mediocre student, the boy who smelled of midnight. He let the meek mask slip, just enough. His posture changed, his shoulders squaring, his chin lifting. He did not look like a frightened noble heir. He looked like a man who had walked through burning cities and felt nothing.
