# Part 23: The Oracle’s Truth

NARRATOR:
He stood, and the darkness rose with him, but it was no longer a flood. It was a river, flowing through him, around him, shaped by his will. The silver ring on his finger blazed, not with borrowed light, but with his own—the light of a promise kept, a future chosen, a world built from ashes.

The Oracle stumbled back, her face contorted. “You’re destroying it! The darkness—the Unmaking—you’re—”

“I’m giving it a purpose.” He looked at the followers, still on their knees, their faces wet with tears, their hands raised in supplication. “You wanted something to believe in. Something that would give your lives meaning. I can’t give you that. No one can. But I can give you a choice.”

He raised his hand, and the darkness flowed from him, not into the world, but into the ground, into the stone, into the bones of the fortress. The black light softened, warmed, became something that was not light or dark but something between—the gray of dawn, the silver of twilight, the color of a world that was still being born.

“This is what the twice-crowned king was meant to be,” he said, his voice carrying to every corner of the fortress. “Not a destroyer. A builder. Not a conqueror. A creator. The darkness in me was never meant to unmake the world. It was meant to reshape it. To give it form. To make it something new.”

He turned to the Oracle, who was staring at him with eyes that held three hundred years of waiting, three hundred years of faith, three hundred years of terrible, beautiful devotion.
