# Part 16: The First Stone

NARRATOR:
The human army arrived on the seventh day, just as Vex'ahlia had predicted.

Kaelen watched them come from the highest tower of the Academy—a spire that had once belonged to the Church, now abandoned, its silver sigils faded. Below, the borderlands stretched to the horizon, a scarred no-man's-land of ash and thorn, and across it, the banners of the human king advanced like a tide of steel and gold.

Ten thousand men. Maybe more. The largest army assembled since the truce was signed.

Beside him, Seraphina's breath caught. "That's not a punitive force. That's an invasion."

"He's not coming to reclaim the Academy," Kaelen said quietly. "He's coming to end it. To make sure there's nothing left for the demons to claim."

Rin crouched on the parapet, her orange eyes narrowed. "We have maybe two thousand fighters. Students, faculty, a few demon lords who decided to stay. Against that?" She shook her head. "We can't win a straight fight."

"Then we don't fight straight." Kaelen's voice was calm, measured. "We don't need to defeat his army. We need to make him question why he's here."

Elara emerged from the stairwell, a scroll in her hand, her face pale. "I found something. In the old archives. A record of the original truce negotiations." She unrolled the scroll, its edges crumbling, its ink faded. "The human king didn't sign the truce because he wanted peace. He signed it because his treasury was empty and his generals were mutinying. The truce wasn't a treaty. It was a ceasefire."

Seraphina's eyes widened. "You're saying the war never ended. It just… paused."
