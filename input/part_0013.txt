# Part 4: The Glutton’s Appetite

NARRATOR:
The healers’ wing was a sterile contradiction—white marble floors warmed by demon-forged braziers, Church-sanctioned medical texts stacked beside jars of demonic ichor. Kaelen sat on a cot while a tired-looking healer with half-moon glasses waved a diagnostic crystal over his chest.

“Your mana channels are… unusual,” she murmured, frowning. “The resonance fluctuation was likely caused by a minor blockage. I’ll prescribe a stabilizing tincture. Take it twice daily.”

Kaelen accepted the small vial with a grateful nod, already knowing he would never drink it. The “blockage” was the seal, and the last thing he needed was Church-sanctioned medicine interfering with whatever game Seraphina was playing.

As he exited the wing, the corridor was empty except for a single figure leaning against the far wall. The blond Inquisition cadet from earlier. His uniform was immaculate, his posture rigid, and his pale blue eyes tracked Kaelen with the patience of a sniper.

“Kaelen Vane,” the boy said. Not a question.

“That’s me.” Kaelen allowed a touch of nervousness into his voice. “Can I help you?”

The boy pushed off the wall and approached, each step measured. “I am Gaius of the Celestial Inquisition. My order monitors… anomalies. The black light from your mana crystal was an anomaly.”

“The healer said it was a channel fluctuation.”

“Healers see bodies. I see souls.” Gaius stopped an arm’s length away. His gaze dropped to Kaelen’s neck—to the spot where Rin had bitten him. “You’ve been marked by a glutton demon. Twice, if I’m not mistaken. And there’s something else on you. Older. Colder.”
