# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
Cool metal a reminder of all the chains he wore. Seraphina's bond. The limiter's cage. The Church's suspicion. Rin's hunger. Elara's curiosity. Vex'ahlia's prophecy.

And now, a woman from his past, coming to decide his future.

He laughed quietly, the sound echoing off the stone walls. Three hundred years ago, he had conquered three realms with nothing but ambition and cruelty. Now, in a body that wasn't his, wearing chains he hadn't chosen, he had to conquer something far more difficult:

He walked back toward the dormitory, his steps slow, his mind already turning. Tomorrow, he would meet Lady Morwen. Tomorrow, he would face the truth of what he had been.

But tonight, he had a choice. He could spend the night planning, calculating, preparing his masks. Or he could spend it the way he had spent the last—with someone who wanted him for no reason he could understand, someone who tasted his power and called it home.

He thought of Rin's smile, her warmth, the way her hand had felt on his chest.

He changed direction, heading not for his dormitory but for the demon quarter.

Rin's room was at the top of a narrow tower, accessible only by a spiral staircase that creaked with every step. The door was unlocked, the room beyond a chaos of discarded clothes, empty plates, and books stacked in precarious towers.

She was sitting on the windowsill, her legs dangling over the edge, looking out at the moon. When he entered, she didn't turn, but her tail curled, a slow, pleased motion.
