# Part 4: The Glutton’s Appetite

NARRATOR:
“No,” Vorg and Kaelen said simultaneously.

The demon faction’s common hall was a stark contrast to the human dormitories—all dark wood, crimson velvet, and braziers that burned with cold blue flame. The demon students lounged on overstuffed couches, playing card games that involved actual blood and arguing about the merits of various tail decorations.

Seraphina awaited him in a private dining chamber, seated at the head of a long table laden with food that smelled far better than anything in the human mess hall. She had changed out of her uniform into a flowing black gown that left her shoulders bare, her silver-blue hair cascading down her back. The candlelight made her golden eyes glow.

“Cousin,” she said, her voice a purr. “Please, sit. We have so much to discuss.”

Rin released Kaelen’s arm and immediately began piling food onto a plate, seemingly unconcerned with formalities. Vorg and the moth-girl—whose name he learned was Lyss—took positions by the door.

Kaelen sat. “I wasn’t aware the Valtiel family claimed the Vane line so closely.”
