# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
He didn't answer. Couldn't answer. The truth was too tangled, too new, too raw to put into words.

Seraphina seemed to understand. She stopped before him, close enough to touch, close enough to smell the roses in her hair. "Morwen came to see me after the evaluation. She told me something I didn't want to hear." She reached up and touched the collar around his neck, her fingers tracing the silver rings. "She said the seal has changed because I've changed. Because when I called you back, I wasn't trying to bind an enemy. I was trying to bring home something I'd lost."

Her hand moved from the collar to his cheek, her palm warm against his skin. "I don't know if that's true. I don't know if what I feel is real or just the echo of my grandmother's pact. But when I'm near you, the blood in my veins sings. When you're gone, it's like the world is quieter. Duller." She leaned in, her forehead almost touching his. "I told you I wanted you to choose me. But I don't know if I have the patience to wait."

He caught her wrist, stilling her hand against his face. "Then don't wait."

Her eyes widened. For a moment, the mask slipped, and he saw something beneath—not the political strategist, not the heir to a bloodline, but a girl who had been waiting for three hundred years for a promise to be kept.
