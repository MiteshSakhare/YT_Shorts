# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
“Once,” the masked inquisitor spat. “Once, three hundred years ago. And that anomaly nearly destroyed the world.”

Vex’ahlia raised a hand. “The tribunal will adjourn for deliberation. The accused will be confined to his dormitory until a verdict is reached. Lady Seraphina will provide a full accounting of the Valtiel bloodline’s magical history. And Captain Valerius…” Her gaze shifted to the scarred captain, who had gone very pale. “…will explain to me why he launched an armed incursion without my authorization.”

She turned to Kaelen, and for a moment, her mask of authority slipped. He saw something in her eyes—calculation, curiosity, and a flicker of something warmer.

“You are not free,” she said quietly. “But you are not condemned. Use the time wisely.”

The dormitory that night was a prison with silk sheets.

Kaelen sat on his bunk, the door locked from the outside, two Church wardens stationed in the corridor. His roommates had been relocated—Cedric to another room, Elara to a separate wing. He was alone, the seal humming softly, the parchment with the prophecy burning a hole in his pocket.

A knock came at the window.

He turned. Rin was perched on the sill, her auburn hair wind-tangled, her grin feral. She tapped the glass with one finger, and it slid open without a sound.

“They really should have put you on a higher floor,” she said, swinging her legs inside. “Third story is nothing for a glutton demon with a reason to climb.”

“You’ll get in trouble.”

“I live in trouble.” She landed on the floor in a crouch, then straightened, her eyes scanning the room. “Nice digs. Very… monastic. Where’s the other one? The human girl who smells like old books and secrets?”
