# Part 23: The Oracle’s Truth

NARRATOR:
“I gave him what he needed. What he wanted.” She tilted her head, studying him. “He wanted to be a hero. He wanted to save the world from the monster he saw in you. I simply showed him the path.”

“You used him. You let him become a monster himself.”

“I let him become what he always was.” Her smile didn’t waver. “We all have darkness inside us, Kaelen. Valerius’s darkness was fear. Your darkness…” She stepped closer, her eyes fixed on his chest. “Your darkness is something else. Something ancient. Something that has been waiting to come home.”

She raised her hand, and the darkness in his chest answered.

It surged, a tidal wave of black light that ripped through his control, filling the courtyard, blotting out the sun. The followers fell to their knees, crying out in ecstasy, in terror, in the raw, primal awe of something they had worshipped without ever understanding.

The Oracle stood at the center of it, untouched, her eyes blazing with a light that matched his darkness. “Do you feel it? The hunger? The need to consume, to unmake, to return to the nothing from which you came?” Her voice was no longer gentle. It was the voice of the abyss, the voice of the void that had been waiting for three hundred years. “This is what you were meant to be. This is what the seal was meant to prevent. And now that it’s broken, you will finally become what the prophecy promised.”
