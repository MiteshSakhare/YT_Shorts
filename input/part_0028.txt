# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
Kaelen considered his options. Kill her? Too risky, too loud, too many witnesses. Silence her with threats? Unreliable—she had the look of someone who wouldn’t bend easily. Seduce her? He dismissed the thought with faint disgust. He was not his former generals, who had solved every problem with violence or lust.

“What do you want?” he asked instead.

Elara pulled the blanket tighter around herself, but her voice was steady. “I want to understand. The mana you released during the test—it was unlike anything I’ve ever recorded. It wasn’t corrupt. It was primordial. Older than the Church’s records. Older than the demon lords’ lineages.” She took a step closer. “I want to study you. Not as a heretic. As a specimen.”

“A specimen,” Kaelen repeated flatly.

“A subject. A case study.” She tilted her head, and in the moonlight, he saw the faintest flush on her cheeks. “And maybe… something more. You’re not the only one in this Academy wearing a mask, Kaelen. I’m a baron’s daughter studying demon biology in a human-supremacist institution. My own family thinks I’m a deviant. The Church thinks I’m a heretic-in-waiting. The demons think I’m a spy.” She smiled, thin and brittle. “We’re all pretending to be something we’re not. At least you’re pretending to be weak.”

He studied her for a long moment. The seal was quiet, no warning pulse. She was telling the truth, or something close to it.
