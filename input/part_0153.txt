Kaelen did not block. He did not dodge. He reached out and caught the blade with his bare hand, the silver ring blazing, the power within him meeting the corruption and unmaking it.

The sword shattered.

Not like the king’s blade, which had crumbled to dust. This was an explosion, a detonation of light and shadow that threw them both across the chamber. Kaelen hit the far wall, his ears ringing, his hand smoking, the silver ring on his finger glowing like a brand.

When he looked up, Valerius was on his knees, his armor cracked, his face bloodied, the remnants of the corrupted sword scattered around him like broken glass. He was staring at his hands, at the darkness that was already eating at them, creeping up his arms like veins of shadow.

“What have I done?” he whispered. And for the first time, Kaelen heard the man beneath the mask—the fear, the regret, the horror of a soul that had sold itself for a cause that was never true.

Kaelen crossed the chamber, kneeling beside him. “You let yourself be used. By hatred. By fear. By something that wanted your faith more than it wanted your salvation.”

Valerius looked up, his eyes clearing for a moment. “I saw you. In the courtyard. The day you stopped the army.” His voice cracked. “I saw you and I knew—I knew you weren’t what I said you were. But it was too late. I’d already become what I was trying to destroy.”
