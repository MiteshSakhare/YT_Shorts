# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
“I’m going to try.”

They stood together, watching the sun sink below the horizon, the first stars appearing in the darkening sky. Somewhere in the Academy, Rin was probably stealing food from the kitchens. Elara was writing furiously in her notebooks, documenting everything, trying to make sense of the chaos. Vex’ahlia was consolidating her power, preparing for the fight ahead.

And Kaelen—once Demon Emperor, now something new—stood at the center of it all, not as a king, but as a fulcrum. A point of balance. A man who had learned, at last, that power meant nothing if you had no one to share it with.

He took the ring from his pocket, slipping it onto his finger. It was warm against his skin, a quiet pulse that matched his heartbeat.

“Let’s go home,” he said.

Seraphina smiled. “Which home?”

He looked at the Academy, at the lights beginning to glow in the windows, at the people who had chosen to stay.

“This one,” he said. “For now.”

She took his hand, and they walked together into the gathering dusk, the ring on his finger a quiet promise of things to come.

End of Part 18
