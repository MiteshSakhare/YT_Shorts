# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
Back into the dining chamber, her gown trailing behind her like spilled ink.

Kaelen stood alone in the corridor, the echoes of the confrontation fading into silence. He touched his lips where her thumb had been, feeling the faint tingle of her mana.

Three factions, he thought. Two demonesses. One Inquisition agent. And a seal that binds me to a bloodline that wants to breed me like a prize stallion.

He laughed quietly, shaking his head.

This is going to be interesting.

End of Part 5

Sleep was a luxury Kaelen no longer allowed himself.

He returned to the human dormitory in the early hours, slipping past his snoring roommates to sit on the windowsill, watching the moon trace silver lines across the Academy’s spires. The seal hummed softly, a constant vibration beneath his ribs, and in the quiet, he could almost hear the ancient voice again—the one that had called him a vow-breaker.

Three hundred years ago, he thought. I made a pact with Seraphina’s great-grandmother. But I don’t remember. Why don’t I remember?

In his past life, the Demon Emperor had possessed a perfect memory. He could recall the face of every general he’d broken, every kingdom he’d burned, every alliance he’d forged and shattered. But the final days of his reign were a blur—a fog of betrayal and desperation that ended with a holy sword through his chest. And somewhere in that fog, apparently, he had sold himself to a pact-maker for a second chance.
