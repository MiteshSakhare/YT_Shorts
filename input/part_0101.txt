# Part 14: The Heretic's Coronation

NARRATOR:
Shattered.

Silver fragments rained down around them, tinkling against the stone like broken glass. Valerius stumbled back, his face white, his hands empty. Behind him, the Church cadets stood frozen, their faith cracking as visibly as their captain's sword.

Kaelen opened his hand. The celestial shards had left no mark on his palm—only the faint glow of power receding, the darkness settling back beneath his skin.

"I am Kaelen Vane," he said, his voice carrying across the courtyard, across the burning quarter, across the entire Academy. "I was the Demon Emperor. I am the twice-crowned king. And I am done hiding."

He raised his hand, and the power within him answered.

Black light erupted from his palm, not in a wave of destruction but in a pulse—a shockwave that rippled through the Academy, extinguishing fires, shattering weapons, throwing Church cadets to their knees. It was not an attack. It was a declaration. A claiming.

The wards that had held the truce for a hundred years blazed in answer, their silver light rising to meet the darkness, and for a moment, the Academy was a battleground of light and shadow, old powers clashing in the dawn.

Then the darkness receded, and Kaelen stood alone in the center of the courtyard, his power thrumming beneath his skin, his eyes fixed on the horizon where the sun was beginning to rise.

Behind him, Seraphina stepped out of the shadows, her hand finding his. Rin flanked his other side, her dagger sheathed, her grin fierce. Elara followed, her books abandoned, her face alight with something that might have been awe.

And in the silence that followed, a new voice spoke.
