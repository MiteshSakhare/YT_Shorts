# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
Silver light erupted from its facets, then black, then silver again—a dance of opposites, light and dark, ice and fire, creation and destruction. The observers leaned back in their seats, hands raised against the glare.

Morwen did not move. She stood beside the obelisk, her red eyes fixed on the shifting colors, her expression unreadable.

When the light finally faded, the crystal was no longer dark. It glowed with a soft, steady light—pale silver shot through with veins of deepest black, like moonlight reflected on frozen water.

“Well,” Morwen said quietly. “That is not what I expected.”

She turned to the observers. “The crystal shows a soul twice-forged. The original—the Demon Emperor—is present, but changed. The seal has not merely bound his power. It has merged with it. His darkness is now intertwined with the Valtiel bloodline’s light. They are not two things anymore. They are one.”

She looked at Seraphina, and for the first time, her expression softened. “Child, do you understand what you’ve done? The seal was meant to be a leash. A weapon. A way to control him. But the moment he woke in this body, the seal began to change. To adapt. To become.” She gestured to the glowing obelisk. “This is not a bound demon. This is a new creation. Something that has never existed before.”

Seraphina’s composure cracked. “What does that mean for the bloodline?”
