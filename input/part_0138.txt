# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
Held in the old throne room of the demon quarter—a chamber that had been sealed since the truce was signed, its obsidian pillars carved with the history of a hundred bloodlines. Lord Malachar sat at the head of the table, his scaled face impassive, his ancient eyes fixed on Kaelen as he entered.

Around him, a dozen other lords and ladies—some with horns, some with wings, some with features that shifted and flowed like water—watched with the cold calculation of creatures who had survived centuries by being patient.

Seraphina walked beside Kaelen, her head high, her presence a shield. Rin and Elara waited outside—this was not a gathering for gluttons or scholars. But Vex’ahlia was already there, seated at the far end of the table, her hands folded before her, her expression neutral.

“Kaelen Vane,” Malachar said, his voice a low rumble. “Or should I say, Demon Emperor?”

“Kaelen is fine.” He took the seat opposite Malachar, Seraphina beside him. “You wanted to see me.”

“We wanted to offer you something.” Malachar’s eyes glittered. “The Valtiel bloodline has waited three hundred years for the return of their king. Seraphina has bound herself to you. The prophecy names you twice-crowned. We would see you crowned properly. Formally. As ruler of the demon realms.”

The words hung in the air. Seraphina’s hand tightened on Kaelen’s under the table, but she said nothing.

“I’m not interested in a crown,” Kaelen said.

Malachar’s expression didn’t change. “You would refuse?”
