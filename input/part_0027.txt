# Part 6: The Taste of Power

NARRATOR:
I intended to break it. I always intended to break it. The thought brought a grim satisfaction. The question is: what did she take from me in exchange?

He closed his eyes and reached inward again, not pushing against the seal but listening. The frozen ocean stirred. Beneath the ice, something moved—vast and coiled, like a serpent of pure darkness. His power. But wrapped around it, chains of light, each link inscribed with names. Valtiel names. Seraphina’s bloodline, binding him strand by strand.

An investment, she had called him. And I fully intend to collect.

A floorboard creaked.

Kaelen’s eyes opened. Elara stood at the foot of his bunk, wrapped in a thin blanket, her dark curls a wild halo around her face. She wasn’t looking at him—she was staring at the window, where frost was spreading across the glass in intricate, fractal patterns.

“You’re doing that,” she said quietly. “The cold.”

He looked at the frost. It was beautiful, organic, the kind of ice that formed in the presence of true demonic power. He hadn’t even noticed.

“I’m not feeling well,” he said, the lie automatic.

“You’re not feeling human,” she corrected, finally meeting his eyes. Her gaze was sharp, intelligent, devoid of fear. “I’ve been studying demonic physiology for three years. I know what mana corruption looks like. I know what a binding seal looks like. And I know you’re not Kaelen Vane.”

The frost on the window thickened, crackling.
