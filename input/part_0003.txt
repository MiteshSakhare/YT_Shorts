# Part 1: The Fool’s Last Bow

NARRATOR:
“I will endeavor to be worthy, Father,” Kaelen said, bowing his head. Inside, the old Emperor stirred. The Academy. It was the perfect place to begin. Not as a dull-witted noble, but as the true player he was. He just needed to unlock the power coiled within him. A power that felt… different. Contained by a strange, unseen barrier he’d never encountered in his previous life.

Later that night, in his spartan quarters, Kaelen sat before a mirror. The reflection showed a boy with stark white hair and tired, violet eyes—a stark contrast to his past self’s raven locks and burning crimson gaze. He looked frail. Pathetic.

He reached inward, feeling for the core of his demonic energy. A vast, frozen ocean lay beneath a brittle sheet of ice. He pushed against it, and a sharp, stabbing pain lanced through his skull. A memory, not his, surfaced. A woman in white robes, her face obscured, whispering over his crib as a baby. A seal. A powerful one, placed to protect… or to imprison?

“Tch,” he scoffed, withdrawing. It would take time. But time was a resource he knew how to exploit.

A soft knock at his door interrupted his meditation. “Young Master? A letter has arrived.”

His personal servant, a quiet girl named Lyra, slipped a cream-colored envelope under the door. He picked it up. The seal was a crest he didn’t recognize—a dragon coiled around a shattered sword. He broke the wax and unfolded the heavy parchment.
