# Part 21: The Trial of Fire

NARRATOR:
The crucible in which the new order was forged—or broken.

Kaelen sat in the gallery of the council chamber, Seraphina on his left, Rin on his right, Elara at his shoulder with her notebooks open. Below, the delegates had assembled in their full regalia: Lord Malachar in obsidian and silver, Lady Ashford in human velvet and gold, Mara in her unadorned white. And in the center, Valerius stood in chains, his scarred face pale, his eyes fixed on the floor.

The charges were read one by one. Incitement to violence. Breaking the truce. Murder of Church clergy. Attempted assassination of a council delegate—Kaelen’s name spoken like a weapon. Each charge was met with evidence, witnesses, testimony. The students who had been in the demon quarter during the purge spoke of fires and terror. The Church acolytes who had witnessed Valerius’s orders gave their accounts with trembling voices. Even Vex’ahlia took the stand, her tarnished-gold eyes cold, her words precise, documenting the chain of command that had led from Valerius’s ambition to the bloodshed.

Throughout it all, Valerius said nothing. He stood in his chains, his face a mask, his hands trembling at his sides. He did not deny the charges. He did not defend himself. He simply… waited.

Kaelen watched him, the darkness in his chest stirring. He had pulled the corruption from Valerius, but the man himself was still a hollow shell, the hatred that had driven him replaced by something that looked like despair.

He’s waiting to die, Kaelen realized. He came here to be a martyr. He still wants to be a symbol.

He leaned forward, his voice cutting through the chamber. “I wish to speak.”
