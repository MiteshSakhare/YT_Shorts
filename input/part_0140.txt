# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
Chair. “You’ve given us much to consider, Kaelen Vane. We will discuss your proposal. In the meantime…” He produced a small box from his robes, sliding it across the table. “A gift. From the demon lords to the man who stopped a war without drawing a sword.”

Kaelen opened the box. Inside, resting on black velvet, was a ring—simple silver, unadorned, but pulsing with a warmth that he recognized immediately. It was made from the same power as the seal, the same power that bound him to Seraphina, but transformed. Not a chain. A promise.

“What is it?” he asked.

“A symbol,” Malachar said. “A reminder that you are not alone. That the demon realms stand with you, whether you wear a crown or not.”

Kaelen closed the box, slipping it into his pocket. “Thank you. I’ll wear it when the time is right.”

Malachar inclined his head. “We will await your summons for the council. In the meantime…” His eyes flicked to Seraphina, then back to Kaelen. “Take care of her. She’s waited long enough.”

Outside, the sun was setting, painting the Academy in shades of orange and red. Kaelen stood on the steps of the demon quarter, Seraphina beside him, the ring box heavy in his pocket.

“That went better than I expected,” she said.

“They’re scared. They don’t know what comes next, and they’re looking for something to hold onto.” He looked at her. “I can’t be that for them. But I can help them build something that is.”

She leaned into him, her head on his shoulder. “You’re going to change the world, aren’t you?”
