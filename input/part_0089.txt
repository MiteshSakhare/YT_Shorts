# Part 12: The Gathering Storm

NARRATOR:
He found them all in Seraphina’s chambers—Rin sprawled on the bed, Elara at the desk with her books, Seraphina standing by the fire. They looked up when he entered, and he saw the question in their eyes: What happened?

He held up the key.

“The Church is going to move against the demon faction. Valerius is planning a purge. And Vex’ahlia just gave me the means to remove my collar.”

Silence. Then Rin was on her feet, her dagger in her hand. “When?”

“Days. Maybe less.”

Seraphina’s face was pale, but her voice was steady. “I’ll call the demon lords. Warn them. Prepare—”

“No.” Kaelen’s voice cut through her words. “If you warn them, they’ll act. And if they act, the truce shatters. The Academy becomes a battlefield. Students die.”

“And if we do nothing?” Elara’s voice was quiet, but her eyes were sharp. “If Valerius strikes first, the same thing happens. But the demons are caught off guard. More deaths.”

Kaelen walked to the center of the room, the key in his palm. “We need to change the game. Valerius is waiting for a provocation. An excuse to strike. So we give him one. But not on his terms. On ours.”

Seraphina’s eyes narrowed. “What are you planning?”

He looked at each of them in turn—the demon heir, the glutton, the scholar. Three women who had chosen him, who saw something in him that he was only beginning to see in himself.
