The mess hall was chaos of a different kind.

Students from all three factions crowded the long tables, their voices a cacophony of languages and laughter. The food was simple—bread, cheese, the last of the preserved meat—but no one seemed to mind. They were alive. The Academy was standing. And for the first time in a hundred years, there were no walls separating demon from human from Church.

Kaelen sat at a corner table, Seraphina on one side, Rin on the other, Elara across from him. They ate in comfortable silence, watching the crowd, feeling the fragile hope that had settled over the Academy like a blessing.

A shadow fell over the table. Vex’ahlia stood there, her silver hair pulled back, her tarnished-gold eyes tired but clear. She was still in her battle leathers, a sword at her hip, but there was something relaxed in her posture that Kaelen hadn’t seen before.

“You’re a difficult man to find,” she said.

“I wasn’t hiding.”

“No.” She slid into the seat beside Elara, accepting a piece of bread from Rin’s outstretched hand. “You were enjoying your victory. As you should.” She bit into the bread, chewing thoughtfully. “The demon lords’ council is at noon. They’re expecting you.”

“I’ll be there.”

“Good.” She looked at him, her expression unreadable. “I’ve been in contact with the Church’s high council. They’re… divided. Mara is pushing for reform. Valerius’s faction is calling for your excommunication. And the human king has sent an envoy requesting a formal audience.”

Kaelen’s jaw tightened. “Already?”
