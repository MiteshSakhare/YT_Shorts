# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
After a long silence, she spoke again. “Seraphina played her hand well tonight. Claiming you as bloodline, presenting the ‘blessing’ as a family matter. She’s trying to own you legally now, not just magically.”

“Vex’ahlia is playing a longer game. She didn’t condemn you, but she didn’t free you either. She’s waiting to see which way you fall.”

“I know that too.”

“And me?” Rin’s grip on his hand tightened. “What am I playing?”

He turned his head to meet her gaze. “I don’t know yet.”

She smiled, slow and warm. “Good. That’s the right answer.”

She shifted closer, her body curving against his side, her head resting on his shoulder. Her hair smelled of smoke and something sweet. “I’m staying here tonight. Guards won’t check until dawn. And I’m not letting you face tomorrow alone.”

He should have refused. Should have sent her back into the night, maintained the distance that kept his options open. But the seal was quiet, and her warmth was real, and for one night, he let himself be human.

“Stay,” he said.

She pressed closer, her lips brushing his jaw. “Wasn’t planning on leaving.”

He closed his eyes, and for the first time in three centuries, he slept without dreaming.

End of Part 8
