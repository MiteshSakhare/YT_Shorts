# Part 15: The Throne of Embers

NARRATOR:
Elara’s voice pulled him from his thoughts. She was sitting across from him in what had become his study—a converted storage room in the neutral zone between the former quarters, its walls lined with her books, its single window looking out over the borderlands.

“Brooding.” She marked her place in a volume of demonic law and set it aside. “You sit in this room, you stare at nothing, and you calculate. I can see the numbers moving behind your eyes.”

He leaned back in his chair. “There’s a lot to calculate. Lord Malachar’s faction wants a formal council with demon representation proportional to military strength. The human ambassador is demanding reparations for damages during the purge. The Church is split—Mara’s faction wants reform, Valerius’s loyalists want revenge. And somewhere in the middle of all of it, there’s a thousand students who just want to finish their education without dying.”

Elara’s lips quirked. “And that’s just the politics. You haven’t even started on the prophecy, the soul-bond, or the fact that three very different women are currently competing for your attention.”

He raised an eyebrow. “Competing?”

“Please.” She stood, walking around the desk to lean against it beside him. “Seraphina wants you to be her king. Rin wants you to be her home. And I…” She tilted her head, her dark curls falling across her face. “I want you to be something the world has never seen. Something that doesn’t fit in any of their categories.”

She reached out and touched the streak of black in his white hair—the mark the unbinding had left. “They see you as a ruler. I see you as a question. An answer that hasn’t been written yet.”
