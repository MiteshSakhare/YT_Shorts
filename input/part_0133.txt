# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
Strand of hair from her face. “What do you want us to be?”

She was quiet for a long moment. “I wanted you to be my weapon. My tool. The thing that would make the Valtiel name unassailable.” She traced the line of his collarbone, her touch light. “Now I don’t know what I want. I just know that when you walked out to face the king, I was ready to burn the world if you didn’t come back.”

“That’s not healthy.”

“No.” She laughed, soft and a little sad. “But it’s true.”

He sat up, pulling her with him, so they were face to face, knees touching. “I spent three hundred years not letting anyone close. I told myself it was strength. It was fear. I was afraid that if I let someone in, they’d see what I really was and leave.” He took her hand, pressing it to his chest. “You already know what I was. You stayed. Rin stayed. Elara stayed. That’s not nothing.”

Seraphina’s eyes glistened. “What are you saying?”

“I’m saying I don’t know what we are. But I know I want to find out. With you. With all of you.” He smiled, a little crooked. “If that’s something you want.”

She kissed him then, and it was different from the kisses before—not desperate, not claiming, but something softer. A beginning instead of a demand.
