# Part 14: The Heretic's Coronation

NARRATOR:
He stopped a few feet from Valerius, close enough to see the sweat on the captain's brow, the tremor in his sword hand. "You don't want to arrest me, Captain. You want to kill me. You want to make me a symbol—the heretic who broke the truce, the monster that justified your purge. You want to write the history that says you were the hero who saved the Academy from darkness."

Valerius's face contorted. "You know nothing of what I want."

"I know everything." Kaelen's voice dropped, low and cold. "I was you, once. I burned cities to prove I was righteous. I slaughtered innocents to prove I was strong. I told myself the same lies you're telling yourself now—that the ends justify the means, that the world would thank me for the blood I spilled." He stepped closer, until the tip of Valerius's sword pressed against his chest. "I was wrong. And so are you."

Valerius's hand shook. The celestial light on his sword flickered, guttering like a candle in the wind. Around them, the chaos had frozen—Church cadets and demon students alike watching, waiting, breath held.

"You're a demon," Valerius hissed. "A corruption. A stain that needs to be cleansed."

"No." Kaelen reached out and closed his hand around the blade of Valerius's sword. The celestial light burned against his palm, but the power within him answered—black light meeting silver, the two forces grinding against each other like tectonic plates. "I'm what you made me. I'm what this Academy made me. I'm what three hundred years of fear and hatred and broken promises made me."
