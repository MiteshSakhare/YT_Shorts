# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
Courtyard, her face pale, her sword drawn. Behind her, the defenders of the Academy stood ready—Rin with her gluttons, Elara with her wards, Vex’ahlia with a squad of Church loyalists.

“Kaelen.” Seraphina’s voice cracked. “We felt the breach. We thought—”

“It’s over.” He stopped before her, letting her see him, whole and alive. “Valerius is in the ritual chamber. He’s alive. The council will decide what happens to him.”

She stared at him, her sword lowering. “You let him live?”

“He was being used. By something older than any of us. I took the darkness from him. It’s in me now.”

Her face went white. “Kaelen—”

“I can contain it.” He took her hands, pressing them to his chest, where the new darkness pulsed alongside the old. “I’ve carried worse. I’ll carry this too, until we find a way to destroy it.”

She searched his face, her eyes bright with unshed tears. “You’re not going to let anyone else carry anything, are you? You’re going to take it all on yourself, like you always do.”

“Not all.” He smiled, tired but genuine. “That’s what I have you for.”

She kissed him then, fierce and desperate, and he held her, the darkness in his chest quieting, the silver ring on his finger pulsing in time with her heartbeat.

When she pulled back, Rin was there, her dagger sheathed, her orange eyes sharp. “Valerius is secure. Elara’s already documenting everything for the council. Vex’ahlia’s people are sweeping the tunnels for any other surprises.”

She stepped closer, her hand finding his, her fingers cold but steady. “You saved him. After everything he did. Why?”
