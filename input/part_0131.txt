# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
Notebook open, her hand shaking as she wrote. "The king's sword," she said, her voice wondering. "How did you—"

"I didn't break it." Kaelen looked at his hand, remembering the feel of the blade crumbling beneath his fingers. "It broke itself. It was never meant to be drawn. It was a symbol, not a weapon. And when the man holding it realized what he was doing, it shattered."

Elara stared at him, then at her notebook, then back at him. "You're saying the sword broke because the king's conviction broke?"

"I'm saying that wars don't end when one side wins. They end when the people fighting them realize they don't want to fight anymore."

He looked at the courtyard, at the students who had gathered to watch, at the defenders who had been ready to die for a place that had never wanted them. They were crying, laughing, embracing each other—demon and human and Church alike, the old divisions forgotten in the sudden, overwhelming relief of survival.

"This is the first day," Kaelen said quietly. "Tomorrow, the real work begins. Rebuilding. Negotiating. Making something that lasts." He looked at Seraphina, at Rin, at Elara. "But tonight… tonight, we rest. Tonight, we remember why we fought."

He walked into the crowd, and they parted for him, and for once, he didn't feel like a heretic or a king or a weapon. He felt like a man who had been given a second chance and had used it to build something worth keeping.
