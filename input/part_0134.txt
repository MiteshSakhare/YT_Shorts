# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
Cheeks were flushed, her composure cracked. “The Demon Emperor, sharing. Who would have thought?”

“I’m not the Demon Emperor anymore.”

“No.” She touched the black streak in his hair. “You’re something better.”

They found Rin and Elara in the old study, the one that had become their unofficial gathering place. Rin was sprawled across the cushions, eating something that looked like candied fire, while Elara sat at the desk, her notebooks spread before her, her quill scratching furiously.

Rin looked up when they entered, her orange eyes sharp. “Finally. I was starting to think she’d eaten you.”

Seraphina’s eyebrow rose. “Jealous?”

“Always.” Rin’s grin was unrepentant. “But I share. Gluttons are generous.”

Elara looked up from her writing, her expression distracted. “The king’s army is in full retreat. Scouts report they’ve crossed the border back into human territory. No signs of regrouping.”

“And Valerius?” Kaelen asked.

Elara’s face tightened. “No sign. He disappeared after the king’s surrender. Some of his loyalists went with him. The rest…” She hesitated. “The rest have been taken into custody by Vex’ahlia. She’s conducting her own investigation.”

“Good.” Kaelen sat on the edge of the desk, looking at the maps Elara had spread out. “What about the demon lords?”

Seraphina moved to his side. “Lord Malachar is convening a council. He wants to formalize the Academy’s status as neutral ground. He’s also…” She paused. “He’s asking for you to attend.”

“He wants to offer you something. A title. A position. A—” She stopped, her jaw tightening.
