# Part 15: The Throne of Embers

NARRATOR:
“A week. Maybe less.” She looked at him, her expression unreadable. “The king will march. He has to. If he doesn’t, his nobles will see it as weakness. The demon lords will mobilize in response. And what you built here—this fragile, beautiful thing—will be ground to dust between them.”

He stared at the moon, at the silver light on the borderlands, at the world that was already preparing to tear itself apart again.

“What do you suggest?”

“I suggest you stop waiting for permission.” She turned to face him fully. “You are the twice-crowned king. You have the power to reshape the world. You have the loyalty of three factions. You have people who love you.” Her voice softened. “What you don’t have is a crown. And until you take one, the old powers will keep fighting over the pieces.”

He met her eyes. “You want me to claim the throne.”

“I want you to stop pretending you’re not already sitting on it.” She reached out and touched the streak of black in his hair. “You are the king the prophecy spoke of. Not because of your power. Because of what you’ve done with it. You ended a war without starting one. You brought enemies together. You made people believe in something other than fear.”

She let her hand fall. “The question is: will you let them keep believing? Or will you let the old world crush them while you wait to be invited?”
