# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
Moment, the darkness receded, and he was just a man with a woman in his arms, the weight of the world set aside.

The next morning, Kaelen woke to find Rin perched on the windowsill of his chambers, her orange eyes sharp, her auburn hair wild.

“You’ve been avoiding me,” she said without preamble.

He sat up, rubbing his eyes. “I’ve been busy.”

“You’ve been carrying something you won’t share.” She slid off the sill, landing beside the bed in a crouch. “I can taste it on you. The darkness. It’s gotten stronger.”

He didn’t deny it. “Vex’ahlia found something. The darkness was split. Half in me, half wandering. Now it’s all in one place again.”

Rin’s face went pale. “That’s not supposed to happen.”

She was quiet for a moment, her hands clenching and unclenching at her sides. Then she climbed onto the bed, straddling his lap, her hands pressing flat against his chest.

“What are you doing?”

“Tasting.” Her eyes closed, her head tilting back. “The darkness. It’s hungry. It wants something. But it’s not just destruction. It’s…” She frowned, her brow furrowing. “It’s incomplete. Like a body missing its soul.”

She opened her eyes, and there was something in them that he hadn’t seen before—fear, yes, but also a strange, fierce hope. “The prophecy. The twice-crowned king. Maybe it doesn’t mean two crowns. Maybe it means two halves. You and the darkness. The emperor and the hunger. When they come together…”
