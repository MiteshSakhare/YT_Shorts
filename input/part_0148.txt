# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
“Everyone has.” She didn’t look up from her writing. “I’m documenting it. For the record. So when this is over, people will know what happened. Who did what. Who chose peace and who chose violence.”

He reached across the table, covering her hand with his. “You’re shaking.”

She looked up then, and he saw the fear she’d been hiding. “He’s going to come here, isn’t he? Valerius. He’s going to attack the Academy.”

“And if he does?” Her voice cracked. “We barely stopped the king’s army. And that was with walls, with wards, with three factions united. If Valerius brings his followers, if he has the element of surprise—”

“Then we’ll stop him.” Kaelen’s voice was calm, steady. “We have something we didn’t have before. We have each other. We have the council. We have people who believe in what we’re building.”

“Do you?” She searched his face. “Do you really believe it?”

He squeezed her hand. “I have to. Because if I don’t, then everyone who died, everyone who fought, everyone who chose to stay—it was all for nothing. And I can’t accept that.”

She was quiet for a moment. Then she stood, walking around the table, and sat on his lap, her arms around his neck, her face buried in his shoulder.

“I’m scared,” she whispered.

“I know.” He held her, feeling her tremble. “So am I.”

“You’re not supposed to be scared. You’re the twice-crowned king.”

“I’m a man who’s been given more chances than he deserves. And I’m terrified of losing them.” He stroked her hair. “But fear isn’t weakness. It’s the thing that keeps us fighting.”

She pulled back, looking at him with red-rimmed eyes. “When did you get so wise?”
