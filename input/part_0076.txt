# Part 11: The Weight of Want

NARRATOR:
One evening, as they sat on the roof of the demon quarter, watching the sun bleed orange across the sky.

"Obviously." She was eating something that looked like candied fruit but smelled like raw magic, her legs dangling over the edge. "I told you. You taste like home. You think I'm going to let that go just because you're complicated?"

"Complicated is a generous word."

She laughed, leaning into him. "Terrifying, then. Ancient. Cursed. Bound to a woman who wants to put her heirs in your belly." She popped another piece of fruit into her mouth, chewing thoughtfully. "Still tastes like home."

He looked at her profile—the wild hair, the freckles, the small scar on her chin. She was beautiful in a way that had nothing to do with the precise, sculpted beauty of Seraphina. Rin was chaos made flesh, appetite without cruelty, hunger without malice.

"Why me?" he asked. "There are other sources of power in this Academy. Other people who could feed you."

She was quiet for a moment, her orange eyes fixed on the horizon. "When I was little, my mother used to tell me that glutton demons are born with a hole in their souls. That's why we're always hungry. We're trying to fill something that was never finished." She turned to look at him, her expression uncharacteristically serious. "I've tasted a lot of mana. Good mana, bad mana, old mana, new mana. It all fills the hole for a little while, but then it drains away, and I'm hungry again."

Which brings us back to... "You're getting attached," he said...
