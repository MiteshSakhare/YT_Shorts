# Part 14: The Heretic's Coronation

NARRATOR:
He stopped in the center of the courtyard, the rising sun casting his shadow long across the stones. "I'm going to walk out of this Academy. I'm going to stand between the factions and dare them to cross me. And when they see that I'm not the enemy they've been told to fear, maybe—just maybe—they'll realize that the only enemy was the fear itself."

He turned to Seraphina. "Can you get the demon students out? Safely?"

She nodded, her eyes bright. "The tunnels. They lead to the border."

He turned to Elara. "The human students. The ones who don't want to fight. Can you give them a path?"

She swallowed, then straightened. "The library has emergency exits. Old passages from before the truce. I can lead them."

He turned to Rin. "The Church cadets who are having doubts. Valerius's people. Can you give them a reason to stop fighting?"

Rin's grin was sharp. "I can make them very, very hungry for something other than blood."

He turned finally to Vex'ahlia. "And you? What will you do?"

She smiled—a real smile, warm and sad and something like hope. "I'll make sure Valerius answers for what he's done. And I'll make sure the Church hears the truth, whether they want to or not."

Kaelen nodded. He looked at the rising sun, at the burning Academy, at the faces of the people who had chosen to stand with him.

"Then let's end this."

The walk to the Academy gates was the longest of his life.
