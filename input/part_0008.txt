# Part 3: The Pawn’s First Move

NARRATOR:
Damn, he thought with genuine respect. She really has been waiting.

End of Part 2

Sleep did not come easily that night.

Kaelen lay in his narrow dormitory bed, the coarse sheets a far cry from the silk of his ducal estate, and dissected the revelation like a general studying a conquered map. His body was a Valtiel—cousin to Seraphina, heir to a bloodline that apparently held the key to his seal. The original Kaelen Vane had been a sickly, half-forgotten branch of the family, sent to the Ducal House of Vane as a political ward after his parents’ death. A convenient vessel for a wandering soul.

Too convenient, he thought.

The voice he’d heard from within the seal—ancient, feminine, furious—had spoken of a vow broken, of three centuries in darkness. That was not the voice of a mere bloodline curse. That was something older. Something he had apparently wronged in a past that even his memories as the Demon Emperor did not fully explain.

He filed the mystery away. First priority: survive the Academy. Second: learn the rules of this world’s power. Third: break the seal and reclaim his strength. Fourth: deal with Seraphina Valtiel.

She thinks she’s the hunter, he mused, watching the moonlight trace patterns on the ceiling. But a hunter who announces herself has already become the prey.

The next morning, the Academy’s true nature revealed itself.
