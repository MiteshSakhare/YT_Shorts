# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
She turned then, her orange eyes soft in the moonlight. "Good. I was starting to think you'd spend the night with your books and your plans and your beautiful cousin who wants to put a ring on your soul."

He sat on the edge of her bed, the mattress sinking beneath his weight. "Seraphina told me about Lady Morwen. She knew me. In my past life."

Rin slid off the windowsill and crossed the room, dropping onto the bed beside him. "And you're scared."

"You're scared." She leaned against him, her head on his shoulder. "Good. Fear is useful. It keeps you honest." She reached up and touched the collar around his neck, her fingers tracing the rings. "This thing. Does it hurt?"

"No. It's just there."

"It's ugly." She tugged at it, as if she could pull it off with sheer stubbornness. "I don't like things that cage you. You're supposed to be wild. Like me."

He caught her hand, stilling it. "Rin."

"Kaelen." She smiled, her face inches from his. "Whatever happens tomorrow, whatever Lady Morwen says, whatever the Church does, whatever your cousin schemes—I'm still here. I'm not going anywhere. I don't care if you were a demon emperor or a farmer or a piece of dirt. You taste like home. That's all I need to know."

She leaned forward, her lips brushing his cheek, her breath warm against his skin. "So stop planning for one night. Stop calculating. Stop wearing all your masks." Her hand found his, fingers intertwining. "Just be here. With me."
