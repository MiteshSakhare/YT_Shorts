# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
The sun set behind the Academy’s spires, painting the sky in shades of crimson and gold. Somewhere in the distance, a bell tolled, calling the students to evening prayers.

Kaelen Vane, once the Demon Emperor, twice-crowned king in waiting, walked into the gathering darkness without fear.

Because for the first time in three hundred years, he wasn’t walking alone.

End of Part 7

The tribunal chamber was a theater of power disguised as a courtroom.

Built into the deepest level of the Church quarter, it was a perfect circle of black and white stone, tiered seating rising on all sides like the walls of a colosseum. The ceiling was a dome of stained glass depicting the Celestial Seers—ancient beings of light and judgment—staring down with expressions of eternal condemnation. Braziers of silver flame lined the walls, casting everything in a cold, unforgiving glow.

Kaelen stood at the center of the circle, alone. The accused’s platform was a single disk of white marble, elevated just enough to make him feel exposed from every angle. The seal beneath his uniform was quiet, but he could feel it waiting—watching, like a beast in a cage.

The tiers filled quickly. Students and faculty alike had abandoned their evening routines for the spectacle. Humans packed the eastern section, their faces a mixture of curiosity and fear. Demons claimed the western seats, their horns and tails a silent declaration of solidarity—or perhaps a warning. Church cadets filled the northern gallery in perfect formation, their white robes gleaming like rows of teeth.
