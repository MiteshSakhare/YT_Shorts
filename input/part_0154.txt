# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
The darkness was spreading, eating into his chest, his neck, his face. Kaelen reached out, the silver ring blazing, and laid his hand on Valerius’s shoulder.

“It’s not too late.”

The darkness came willingly, eagerly, leaping from Valerius’s body to Kaelen’s hand, drawn by the power that waited within him. It burned, cold and hungry, but Kaelen had held a frozen ocean for three hundred years. He held this too, shaping it, containing it, pulling it from the man who had been its puppet.

When it was done, Valerius collapsed, his face gray, his breath shallow, but alive. The darkness that had consumed him was gone, sealed within Kaelen’s chest, joining the power that already lived there.

Valerius stared up at him, his eyes wide. “Why?”

“Because I know what it’s like to be consumed by something you can’t control.” Kaelen stood, looking down at the man who had tried to kill him. “Because I was given a second chance, and I want to believe that anyone can be, if they choose to be.”

He turned and walked toward the exit, his steps heavy, the new darkness settling within him like a stone dropped into still water.

“The council will judge you,” he said without looking back. “That’s the world we’re building. A world where justice replaces revenge. If you survive, you’ll have to live with what you’ve done. That’s punishment enough.”

He left Valerius kneeling in the shattered chamber, the silver ring on his finger pulsing with a light that had not been there before, and walked back toward the light.
