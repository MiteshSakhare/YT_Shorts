# Part 2: The Serpent’s Welcome

NARRATOR:
She turned, her tail flicking against his wrist as she walked away, her two companions falling in behind her.

Kaelen stood still, the sting on his cheek a reminder that this world was not his old empire. Here, he had no armies. No power. Only a sealed soul and a game he hadn’t realized he was already losing.

He raised a hand to his cheek. The cut was already healing, but the magic in it lingered—a tracking mark, subtle and complex.

Clever girl, he admitted silently. But you’ve made your first mistake. You’ve shown me your hand before the game has even begun.

He wiped the blood onto his thumb and pressed it to his lips, tasting her mana. It was potent. Wild. And strangely familiar in a way that made the frozen ocean within him crack, just a fraction.

That night, in his assigned dormitory—a modest room he was to share with two other human noble sons—Kaelen sat in the darkness, his roommates’ snoring a dull backdrop. He closed his eyes and for the first time, instead of pushing against the seal, he listened.

From the depths of the frozen ocean, a voice answered. Ancient. Feminine. And utterly furious.

“You broke your vow, little king. You left me in the dark for three centuries. And now you dare to return wearing the flesh of my own bloodline?”

His eyes snapped open. His reflection in the window showed his violet irises flickering, for just a moment, to crimson.

He had not just reincarnated into a random noble house.

He was in the body of Seraphina Valtiel’s cousin. And the seal on his power was not a prison—it was a leash held by her family.
