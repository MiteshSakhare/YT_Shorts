# Part 14: The Heretic's Coronation

NARRATOR:
Academy like a blade through flesh.

By the time Kaelen and the others emerged from the ritual chamber, the corridors were already chaos. Church cadets in white and silver poured through the demon quarter, their swords drawn, their faces masks of righteous fury. Students screamed and scattered. The first fires were already burning—torches thrown through windows, banners torn down, the fragile architecture of the truce crumbling in a single night.

Kaelen stood at the entrance to the tunnels, watching the destruction unfold. His collar was gone. The seal was broken. And the power that had been bound for three centuries moved through him like a second heartbeat, waiting to be unleashed.

Seraphina pressed against his side, her face pale but her voice steady. "Lord Malachar's forces are rallying in the eastern quarter. If we can reach them—"

"There's no time." Kaelen's eyes tracked the movement of Valerius's squad through the chaos. The scarred captain was methodical, efficient, cutting a path through the demon quarter with the precision of a surgeon. "He's not trying to capture. He's trying to provoke. He wants a massacre, something that will force the demon lords to break the truce completely."

"Then what do we do?" Rin's dagger was in her hand, her body coiled to spring. "We can't just stand here."

Kaelen looked at the key in his palm—the demon lords' key, the one Rin had taken from Lord Malachar. The collar was gone, but the keys remained, each one a piece of the old cage. He closed his fist around it, feeling the metal bite into his skin.
