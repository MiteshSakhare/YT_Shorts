# Part 14: The Heretic's Coronation

NARRATOR:
Echoing on the stone, the weight of a thousand eyes on his back. Behind him, Seraphina led the demon students into the tunnels. Elara guided the humans to the library. Rin moved among the Church cadets, her presence a strange comfort, a reminder that hunger could be satisfied without violence.

And ahead, at the gates, the leaders of the three factions waited.

Lord Malachar stood with his demon lords, their horns gleaming, their faces unreadable. The human ambassador clutched her robes, her eyes wide. And at the center, flanked by the remnants of Valerius's squad, stood a figure in white robes—the masked inquisitor from the tribunal, his face hidden, his hands folded before him.

Kaelen stopped before them, the gates looming behind, the rising sun painting the world in shades of gold and crimson.

"You've caused quite a stir," the inquisitor said, his voice distorted by the mask. "The heretic who freed himself. The demon who claims to be a king. The boy who would rather burn bridges than cross them."

"I'm not burning anything," Kaelen said. "I'm giving you a chance to build something new."

"And if we don't want something new?"

Kaelen met the blank white mask without flinching. "Then you'll have to kill me. Here. Now. In front of everyone. And you'll have to explain why a boy who walked out of your prison with his hands empty was such a threat that you had to murder him in the dawn."

The inquisitor was silent for a long moment. Then he laughed—a dry, brittle sound. "You're more dangerous than Valerius ever imagined. Not because of your power. Because of your patience."

Which brings us back to... He walked alone, his footsteps...
