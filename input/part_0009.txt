# Part 3: The Pawn’s First Move

NARRATOR:
The Spire Academy was not merely a school; it was a crucible. The curriculum was split into three pillars: Martial Theory, Mana Weaving, and Political Doctrine. But the unspoken fourth pillar was survival. Students were encouraged—expected—to form factions, to challenge rivals, to prove their worth through bloodless (and sometimes not-so-bloodless) duels. The faculty, a mix of human generals, demon lords, and Church inquisitors, watched with the detached interest of scholars observing a cage match.

Kaelen’s first day was deliberately unremarkable. He attended his classes, answered questions with studied mediocrity, and avoided eye contact with Seraphina, who sat at the demon faction’s front row in Political Doctrine, her golden eyes occasionally flicking toward him with amusement.

But it was in Mana Weaving that the trap sprang.

The class was held in a circular arena beneath the Academy, where students were tested on raw magical output. The instructor, a towering demon with four arms and a bored expression, called names at random. When he called “Kaelen Vane,” a ripple of whispers went through the human students. The Vane name carried weight, but Kaelen’s reputation as the “Dull Duke” preceded him.

He stepped onto the arena floor, feeling the weight of a hundred eyes. Among them, Seraphina’s, sharp and expectant.

The test was simple: channel mana into a crystalline obelisk, which would glow according to output. Most students produced a faint shimmer. Talented ones a steady light. Prodigies made it blaze.
