# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
Home. It was a word he had never thought he would use. But the chambers they shared—a set of rooms in the neutral zone, furnished with things from all three factions—had become exactly that. A place where he could let his walls down. Where he could be Kaelen, not the Demon Emperor, not the twice-crowned king, not the heretic who stopped a war.

“You’re brooding again,” Seraphina said one evening, her voice soft. She was curled beside him on the window seat, watching the sunset paint the borderlands in shades of orange and pink.

“Same thing.” She traced the line of his jaw with her fingertip. “What is it?”

He was quiet for a moment. “Valerius is still out there. The king’s envoy says he’s gone to ground, but he has loyalists. People who believe the war should have been fought. People who see what we’re building and want to tear it down.”

Seraphina’s hand stilled. “You think he’ll come back.”

“I think he never left. Not really.” He turned to look at her. “The council is a beginning, but it’s fragile. One act of violence could shatter it. And Valerius knows that.”

“Then we prepare.” Her voice was firm. “We watch. We wait. And when he shows himself, we end it.”

“Not with violence.”

She raised an eyebrow. “You’ve changed, but you’re not a pacifist.”

“No. But I’m not a murderer either. Not anymore.” He took her hand, pressing it to his chest. “If Valerius comes, we stop him. We bring him to the council. We let them judge him. That’s what we’re building—a world where justice replaces revenge.”
