# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
The council chambers were a compromise.

The room had once been the Church’s tribunal hall—the same circular chamber where Kaelen had stood accused. But the silver braziers had been replaced with neutral flames, the stained-glass ceiling covered with plain white cloth, the tiered seating rearranged into a single circle. No head. No foot. No faction above another.

Kaelen stood at the edge of the circle, watching the delegates arrive. Lord Malachar came first, his scaled face unreadable, his entourage of demon lords trailing behind him like shadows given weight. Then the human envoy—a woman named Lady Ashford, silver-haired and sharp-eyed, sent by King Aldric to salvage what remained of his dignity. Finally, Inquisitor Primus Mara, her masked face turned toward the center of the room, her white robes a stark contrast to the bare stone.

Vex’ahlia was not with the Church delegation. She stood near the wall, her arms crossed, her tarnished-gold eyes fixed on Kaelen. Waiting.

“This is unprecedented,” Lady Ashford said, her voice cool. “A council of three factions, meeting on neutral ground. There is no precedent for this.”

“Then we’ll create one,” Kaelen said. He moved to the center of the circle, his voice carrying to every corner of the chamber. “The old system failed. The truce was a pause, not a peace. We have a chance now to build something that lasts. Something that doesn’t depend on fear or force.”

Mara’s masked face tilted. “And you believe a council of equals will succeed where kings and inquisitors failed?”
