# Part 16: The First Stone

NARRATOR:
Moment. "You want to talk an army out of attacking."

"I want to give them a reason to put down their swords."

"That's the same thing."

"It's the only thing that matters." He reached across the table and took her hand. "I'm not the Demon Emperor anymore. I don't conquer. I don't destroy. I build. And you can't build anything on ground that's soaked in blood."

She looked at their joined hands, then at his face. "You've changed."

"I'm changing." He squeezed her fingers. "I don't know what I'll be when this is over. But I know I don't want to be what I was."

She leaned forward, her forehead almost touching his. "Then let's make sure there's something left to become."

The morning of the fourth day, the human army made camp within sight of the Academy walls.

Kaelen stood on the battlements, watching the tents rise like mushrooms after rain, the banners of a hundred noble houses snapping in the wind. At the center of the camp, a pavilion of gold and white marked the king's command—a man Kaelen had never met but already knew: Aldric the Bold, called the Lion of the South, the man who had spent a decade consolidating power, waiting for the moment to strike.

"Impressive," a voice said behind him.

He turned. Vex'ahlia stood at the top of the stairs, her silver hair pulled back, her tarnished-gold eyes fixed on the army. She was dressed in battle leathers for the first time—gray, unadorned, but fitted to her like a second skin.
