# Part 15: The Throne of Embers

NARRATOR:
He was silent for a long moment. Then he turned back to the window, his hands braced on the sill, his eyes fixed on the horizon where the human armies would soon march.

“I need time,” he said finally. “I need to prepare. To build something that can survive what’s coming.”

“You have a week.”

“Then a week is enough.”

He looked at her reflection in the glass—her silver hair, her sharp eyes, the ghost of a smile on her lips. “Will you stand with me? When the armies come?”

She stepped closer, her reflection beside his. “I’ve been standing with you since the moment you walked out of that tribunal with a collar around your neck.” She placed her hand on his shoulder, her touch light. “I’m not going anywhere.”

They stood together at the window, watching the moon trace silver lines across the borderlands, and Kaelen felt something settle in his chest. Not peace—peace was a luxury he couldn’t afford. But purpose. A direction. A reason to keep fighting.

The armies were coming. The war that had been building for a hundred years was finally here.

But for the first time, he didn’t face it alone.

End of Part 15
