# Part 14: The Heretic's Coronation

NARRATOR:
His mask.

The face beneath was old, lined with centuries, the eyes the color of weathered stone. A woman, Kaelen realized. The masked inquisitor had been a woman all along.

"I am Inquisitor Primus Mara," she said, her voice no longer distorted, but soft, almost gentle. "And I have been watching you since the moment you arrived. Not as a threat. As a possibility."

She stepped forward, and the demon lords tensed, but she raised a hand, stopping them. "The Church has been waiting for someone like you for a thousand years. Not a heretic to burn. A heretic to listen to. Someone who could see what we've become and tell us the truth without flinching."

She stopped before him, close enough to touch. "You could be that person, Kaelen Vane. You could be the bridge between factions. The king who doesn't conquer, but builds. The twice-crowned monarch who wears a crown of thorns and a crown of stars and chooses to lay them both down for something better."

She held out her hand. "What do you say? Will you be our heretic? Our king? Our hope?"

Kaelen looked at her hand. He thought of the frozen ocean within him, the chains that had bound him, the people who had chosen him not for what he was but for what he might become.

He took her hand.

"I'll try," he said. "That's all I can promise. I'll try to be something other than what I was. I'll try to build something other than ashes. I'll try to be the king you're asking for."

Mara smiled. "That's more than most kings ever promise."
