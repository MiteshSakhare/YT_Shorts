# Part 4: The Glutton’s Appetite

NARRATOR:
Kaelen didn’t move. Didn’t flinch. “Then why haven’t you?”

She laughed, soft and dangerous. “Because I don’t want a slave. I want a king. The Academy is a game of factions, cousin. The humans, the Church, and us demons—we’re balanced on a knife’s edge. One wrong move and the truce shatters. War returns. And in war, everyone loses.”

She walked back to her seat, settling into it with the grace of a queen. “I want you to help me break that balance. Not as my servant. As my equal. Help me unite the demon houses under the Valtiel banner, and I’ll remove your seal piece by piece. You’ll regain your power, your memories, your glory. And in return…” Her golden eyes locked onto his. “You’ll give me what you promised her. An heir worthy of the Valtiel name.”

The words hung in the air. Rin stopped chewing, her eyes wide. Even Vorg and Lyss exchanged glances.

Kaelen finally allowed a genuine smile to cross his lips. It was not the smile of the meek Duke’s son. It was the smile of the Demon Emperor, cold and calculating and utterly without mercy.

“You want me to help you win a political game,” he said slowly. “And in exchange, you’ll give me back my power. But the moment I’m strong enough, what’s to stop me from simply taking what I want and leaving you with nothing?”
