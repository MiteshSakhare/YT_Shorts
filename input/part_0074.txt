# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
She walked off the platform, her small form disappearing through a side door without a backward glance.

The chamber was silent. The demon lords exchanged glances. The human representative was pale. Vex’ahlia’s expression was unreadable, but her tarnished-gold eyes were fixed on Kaelen with a new intensity.

Seraphina rose from her seat. Her face was calm, but her hands were shaking as she walked onto the platform, stopping inches from him.

“She’s wrong about one thing,” she said quietly.

“She said the seal binds you to my bloodline. To Rin. To Elara.” She reached up and touched the collar around his neck, her fingers cold against his skin. “But it binds me to you, too. And I don’t know if that’s a leash or a lifeline.”

She leaned in, her lips brushing his ear. “When this is over—when the Academy, the factions, the war that’s coming—when all of it is over, I’m going to find out what kind of man you’ve become. And then I’m going to collect what my grandmother was promised.”

She pulled back, her golden eyes blazing. “Don’t disappoint me, Emperor.”

She walked away, her gown trailing behind her, leaving Kaelen alone on the platform with the glowing obelisk and the weight of a thousand watching eyes.

He touched the collar again, feeling the three keys that held it closed. Seraphina. Vex’ahlia. The demon lords. All of them holding pieces of his cage.

But Morwen’s words echoed in his mind: The only choice you have is whether to stand beside him or be swept away when he rises.
