# Part 2: The Serpent’s Welcome

NARRATOR:
Written in elegant, arrogant script:

“I know what you are, Demon Emperor. Don’t bother trying to hide at the Academy. I’ll be waiting.”

There was no signature. Only the scent of roses and something else. Something that made the ancient, ruthless mind of Kaelen Dragomir, for the first time in centuries, feel a prickle of genuine unease.

Someone in this new world knew his true identity. And they were inviting him to a game.

End of Part 1

The week passed in a blur of forced etiquette lessons, tailor-fittings for Academy uniforms, and a seemingly endless parade of noble relatives who came to offer the Duke’s heir “encouragement” that dripped with condescension. Kaelen played his role perfectly: the quiet, unremarkable boy who smiled too softly and apologized too quickly.

But every night, he studied the letter. The paper was infused with a faint magical signature—a type of mana he’d never encountered in his former empire. It was like trying to read a language written in scents rather than words. He learned only one thing: the writer was female.

Intriguing, he thought. And potentially useful.

The morning of his departure, Duke Arcturus summoned him to the grand study. The firelight cast long shadows across the room’s military regalia and hunting trophies. His father stood by the window, back turned.

“You will not shame this house, Kael,” the Duke said without turning. “The Academy has three factions: the Human Supremacists, the Church’s Inquisition cadets, and the Demon Lords’ scions. You will align with none of them. You will be invisible. You will survive.”

Kaelen bowed. “As you command, Father.”
