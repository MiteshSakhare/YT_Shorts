“Why did you summon me?” he asked.

She turned away, walking back to the window. “Because the storm is coming. The truce is cracking. The Church’s hardliners—Valerius and his faction—are pushing for a purge. They have evidence—real or fabricated, it doesn’t matter—that the demon faction is planning to break the truce and seize the Academy. They’re calling for a preemptive strike.”

Kaelen’s blood went cold. “When?”

“Soon. Days, perhaps. They’re waiting for the right moment. A provocation. An excuse.” She looked at him over her shoulder. “You’ve become the center of their narrative. The corrupt mana. The demon consorts. The heretic who walks openly among the faithful. If they move against the demons, they will move against you first.”

“And you? Will you stop them?”

She laughed—a bitter, humorless sound. “I am one woman. The Church is an institution. I have authority, but it has limits. If Valerius forces a vote, I will be outnumbered. If the purge begins, I will be swept aside.” She turned to face him fully. “I didn’t summon you to warn you, Kaelen. I summoned you to ask you a question.”
