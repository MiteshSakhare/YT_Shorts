# Part 17: The Lion's Den

NARRATOR:
He looked at Vex'ahlia. She stepped out of the shadows, her sword at her side, her eyes bright.

"Tomorrow," he said, "we end a war. And we start something new."

End of Part 16

Dawn came cold and gray, the sun struggling to break through a sky heavy with clouds. Kaelen stood at the Academy gates, alone, watching the human camp stir to life. Tents were being struck, soldiers forming ranks, the great engines of war being wheeled into position.

He had sent the others back. Seraphina had argued—fiercely, with fire in her golden eyes—but in the end, she had understood. This was not a battle that could be won with armies. This was a war that had to be ended with a single moment, a single choice, a single man walking into the lion's den and refusing to be afraid.

"I'll be back," he had told her, and she had kissed him—hard, desperate, a promise and a threat all at once.

"You'd better be," she had said. "I didn't wait three hundred years to lose you now."

Rin had simply hugged him, her face buried in his chest, her voice muffled. "If you die, I'll eat your soul. And then I'll be sad forever. Don't make me sad forever."

Elara had pressed a small notebook into his hand—a blank one, its pages waiting. "Write down what happens," she had said. "So we know. So we remember."
