# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
Kaelen stood still as they fitted it around his neck. The metal was cold against his skin, then warm, then present—a weight that settled into his bones, a pressure that pushed against the seal like a hand pressing on a bruise.

The seal screamed in protest. The frozen ocean churned, ice cracking, darkness rising. For a moment, the black light flickered at his fingertips, and the artisans stepped back in alarm.

Then the limiter pulsed—once, twice, three times—and the seal quieted. The darkness receded. The ice reformed.

The human artisan exhaled. "It's done. The limiter is calibrated to your mana signature. If your power exceeds safe thresholds, it will suppress it automatically. If you attempt to remove it, it will alert all three factions simultaneously."

Kaelen touched the collar. It was seamless, the rings interlocked, impossible to remove without the three keys.

Another leash, he thought. But this one, at least, I can use.

He walked out of the fitting chamber and into the corridor beyond, where Seraphina was waiting.

She reached out and touched the collar, her fingers tracing the silver rings. "It suits you," she said quietly. "The chains, I mean. You wear them well."

"I don't intend to wear them long."

"No. You don't." She stepped closer, her hand moving from the collar to his cheek. "But you will wear them for now. And while you do, I'll be working to make sure the keys are held by people who won't use them against you."
