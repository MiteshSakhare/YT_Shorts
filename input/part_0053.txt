# Part 8: The Tribunal

NARRATOR:
“Good.” Rin flopped onto his bunk, sprawling across the blankets. “She was getting too close. You need people who want you, not people who want to study you.”

“You want to study me.”

“I want to taste you.” She patted the space beside her. “Different things.”

He didn’t move. “Rin, you shouldn’t be here.”

“Probably not.” She propped herself up on her elbows, her shirt gaping open at the collar, revealing the pale hollow of her throat. “But I told you I’d be your muscle. And muscles don’t follow rules. They break them.”

She reached out and caught his hand, pulling him down onto the bunk beside her. He resisted for a moment, then let himself fall, the exhaustion of the day finally catching up.

They lay side by side, staring at the ceiling. Rin’s hand was still wrapped around his, her fingers warm, her pulse steady.

“You scared them today,” she said quietly. “The tribunal. The judges. Valerius. You scared them so badly they couldn’t even pretend to be brave.”

“It is good. Fear is useful. But it’s also lonely.” She turned her head to look at him, her orange eyes soft in the darkness. “That’s why I’m here. So you don’t have to be alone with it.”

He didn’t answer. He didn’t have words for what he felt—gratitude, confusion, something that might have been the beginning of trust.
