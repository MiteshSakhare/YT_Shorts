# Part 1: The Fool’s Last Bow

NARRATOR:
A flash of blinding white. The sensation of falling, not through space, but through the very fabric of self.

Kaelen Dragomir, the Demon Emperor, the man who had conquered the three great realms through sheer, merciless strategy and overwhelming power, felt the last threads of his life unravel. He remembered the betrayal—the holy sword driven through his heart by his most trusted general, the one who had whispered of peace. As the light faded, his final thought was not of regret for his empire, but a cold, bitter amusement. All that ambition, undone by a fool’s notion of loyalty.

It wasn’t the pain of death, but the wrenching, tearing sensation of being shoved back into a living body. His eyes snapped open. He wasn’t in his obsidian fortress. He was on a cold, hard floor, staring up at a vaulted ceiling of polished mahogany and crystal chandeliers. The air smelled of dust, old paper, and incense.

He tried to move, but his limbs felt small, weak. Panic, a foreign emotion for the Demon Emperor, clawed at his throat. He looked down. Child’s hands. Soft, uncalloused.

A booming voice echoed in the hall. “Kael! Enough of your daydreaming! The contract with House Valtiel is the only thing keeping our Ducal status afloat, and you dare to skive off your studies to… to nap?!”

Which brings us back to... The Twice-Crowned King...
