# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
But it was enough. Valerius was led away, his face unreadable, his chains clinking on the stone floor.

Kaelen watched him go, the darkness in his chest stirring, and wondered if he had done the right thing.

That night, he stood on the Academy walls, looking out at the borderlands. The moon was full, the stars bright, and somewhere in the darkness, the followers of Valerius were regrouping, waiting for their leader to return.

He felt her approach before she spoke. Vex’ahlia’s steps were always silent, but her presence was unmistakable—a weight in the air, a shift in the shadows.

“You did something today that no one else would have done,” she said, stopping beside him. “You showed mercy to a man who deserved none.”

“Mercy isn’t about what people deserve.”

“No.” She looked at him, her tarnished-gold eyes unreadable. “It’s about what you are.”

She was quiet for a moment, watching the moon trace silver lines across the borderlands. “The darkness you took from him—the corruption in the sword. Do you know what it was?”

“Not yet. But I will.”

“It was old,” she said slowly. “Older than the Church. Older than the demon lords. It was the same darkness that your empire was built on, the same hunger that drove you to conquer three realms.” She met his eyes. “It was yours, Kaelen. The part of you you thought you’d left behind. And now it’s back inside you.”

He touched his chest, where the new darkness pulsed. “I know.”

“Are you afraid of it?”
