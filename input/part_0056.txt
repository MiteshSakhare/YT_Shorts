# Part 9: The Bonds That Bind

NARRATOR:
For a moment, they simply looked at each other—his violet, her orange—and in that moment, there were no factions, no seals, no prophecies. Just two people who had somehow found a moment of peace in the middle of a storm.

Then Rin smiled, slow and lazy. "You're staring again."

"You were in my space."

"Your space is comfortable." She stretched against him, her body arching, her shirt riding up to reveal a strip of pale stomach. "Warm. Safe. You taste like calm in the mornings. Did you know that? Like cool water after a long run."

She sat up, her hair falling around her face, and looked down at him with an expression that was almost tender. "You should sleep like that more often. Without the walls. Without the masks."

He sat up as well, putting distance between them. "I don't have the luxury of letting my walls down."

"Maybe not." She reached out and tucked a strand of white hair behind his ear, her fingers lingering on his cheek. "But maybe you don't have to keep them up with everyone. Maybe you can choose one person. One person who gets to see you when you're not being the Demon Emperor or the meek Duke's son or the twice-crowned king."

She pulled her hand back, her grin returning. "I volunteer, by the way. I'm very good at secrets. Ask anyone."

A knock at the door cut off his response.

"Kaelen Vane." A guard's voice, muffled through the wood. "The tribunal has reached a verdict. You are to present yourself in the main hall immediately."
