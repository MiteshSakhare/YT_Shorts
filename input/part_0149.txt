# Part 19: The Architecture of Peace

NARRATOR:
He smiled, small and tired. “You learn a few things.”

She kissed him then—soft, desperate, a plea and a promise all at once. He held her close, feeling her heartbeat against his chest, the warmth of her body, the trust she was placing in him.

When they broke apart, she was still trembling, but her eyes were clearer. “Whatever happens, I’m with you. All the way.”

“Good.” She rested her head on his shoulder. “Now hold me until I stop shaking.”

He held her, and the library was quiet around them, the books silent witnesses to the fragile, terrifying work of building a future.

The next morning, Kaelen stood on the Academy walls, watching the borderlands. The sun was rising, painting the ash and thorn in shades of gold, and somewhere out there, Valerius was gathering his forces, preparing to strike.

He felt Seraphina come up behind him, her arms wrapping around his waist, her warmth seeping through his coat.

“You’re up early.”

She pressed her cheek against his back. “You’re thinking about what comes next.”

“Good. Because we need you thinking.” She turned him around, facing him. “But you also need to rest. To eat. To let other people carry some of the weight.”

“Do you?” She raised an eyebrow. “Because you look like you haven’t slept in a week.”

He laughed, despite himself. “I’ll try to do better.”

“You will.” She kissed him, quick and firm. “Because I’m going to make sure of it. You’re not alone, Kaelen. You don’t have to carry everything yourself.”

He looked at her—this woman who had once tried to bind him, who had chosen instead to stand beside him. “I’m starting to believe that.”
