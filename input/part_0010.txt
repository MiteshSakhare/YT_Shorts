# Part 3: The Pawn’s First Move

NARRATOR:
The cold surface and reached inward. The frozen ocean stirred. He could feel the seal’s chains, forged from something that resonated with Seraphina’s own mana. If he pushed too hard, she would feel it. If he pushed too little, he would confirm his reputation as a weakling—which might be useful, but would also mark him as prey for the more aggressive students.

He chose a middle path. A controlled trickle of power, just enough to make the obelisk glow a modest blue.

The instructor grunted. “Adequate.”

But as Kaelen withdrew his hand, the obelisk flickered. For a split second, the blue light turned black—a void so absolute that the temperature in the arena dropped. Gasps echoed through the stands. Then it was gone, the crystal clear once more.

The instructor’s four eyes narrowed. “Interesting. A resonance fluctuation. See the healers after class, boy. Your mana channels may be unstable.”

Kaelen bowed, his face a mask of innocent concern. Inside, his mind raced. That black light was his power—the demonic essence he’d wielded as Emperor. The seal had cracked, just a hair, and a sliver had escaped.

He walked back to his seat, acutely aware of the new weight of attention. The human students now looked at him with a mix of pity and curiosity. The demon students with calculation. And the Church cadets—those in white and silver—with something far more dangerous: suspicion.
