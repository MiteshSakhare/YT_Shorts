# Part 24: The Twice-Crowned King

NARRATOR:
Run, didn’t cry, didn’t embrace him. She simply walked up to him, her notebooks clutched to her chest, and said, “I need to document what happened. Every detail. The darkness, the Oracle, the transformation. It’s important.”

He reached into his coat and pulled out the small notebook she had given him, its pages filled with his cramped handwriting. “I wrote it down. So we know. So we remember.”

She took the notebook, her fingers brushing his, and for a moment, her composure cracked. “You came back.”

“I promised I would.”

She nodded, tucking the notebook into her own collection. “Good. Now let’s go home. I’m tired of waiting.”

The Academy that rose from the ashes of the old was not a monument to any one faction. It was a patchwork of all three—human stonework beside demon obsidian, Church silver woven into the gates, the neutral quarter expanded to hold the council chambers, the library, the halls where students from every corner of the world came to learn.
