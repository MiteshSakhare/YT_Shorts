# Part 21: The Trial of Fire

NARRATOR:
Valerius stared at her, his face working. “You’re not going to execute me.”

“No.” Lady Ashford’s voice was firm. “We are not the monsters you believed us to be. We are something new. And something new requires a different kind of justice.”

She sat down, and the chamber was silent. Valerius stood motionless, his hands trembling, his eyes moving from the delegates to the gallery, to Kaelen.

He bowed his head. “I accept.”

The guards led him away, and the delegates began to file out, their faces a mixture of exhaustion and relief. Kaelen remained in the gallery, watching the empty chamber, the weight of what had just happened settling over him.

Seraphina touched his arm. “You did it.”

“We did it.” He looked at her, at Rin, at Elara, at Vex’ahlia standing in the shadows. “All of us.”

Rin grinned. “Does this mean we get to celebrate? Because I’ve been saving a bottle of something I stole from Lord Malachar’s cellars, and it’s been calling my name for days.”

Elara laughed, the sound surprising all of them. “I’ll document it. For posterity.”

“You’ll do no such thing.” Rin grabbed her arm, pulling her toward the door. “What happens in the celebration stays in the celebration. Those are the rules.”

Seraphina took Kaelen’s hand, pulling him after them. “Come on, twice-crowned king. Your kingdom awaits.”

He let her lead him, the darkness in his chest quiet, the silver ring warm on his finger, the laughter of the women he loved echoing in the corridors of the Academy.

It was not peace. Not yet. But it was something. Something worth fighting for.

End of Part 21
