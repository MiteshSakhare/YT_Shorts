“I didn’t handle anything,” Kaelen said quietly. “I simply stood still while three factions tripped over each other to claim me. That’s not strategy. That’s luck.”

“Luck is a strategy,” Rin chimed in, appearing on his other side. “The best kind, really. Now can we go back inside? I didn’t finish my dessert.”

Kaelen turned to look at her, and for the first time, he noticed the state of her uniform—or rather, the state of her. The confrontation had torn her shirt at the shoulder, revealing a pale collarbone and the strap of something dark beneath. Her hair was wild, her lips slightly parted, and she was watching him with the same hungry intensity she’d shown when she bit him.

“You’re staring,” she said, her voice dropping to a husky murmur.

“I’m assessing,” he corrected, looking away. “There’s a difference.”

“Is there?” She laughed, low and warm. “Because from where I’m standing, it looked an awful lot like staring.”
