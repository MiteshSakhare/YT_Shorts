# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
“The truth.” Morwen laughed, a bitter, broken sound. “The Demon Emperor, asking to be believed.” She withdrew her hand, stepping back. “Seraphina thinks you can be redeemed. She thinks the bond will force you to become something better, something worthy of her bloodline. She’s young. She doesn’t understand that power doesn’t change people—it just shows them what they already are.”

She turned to leave, then paused at the doorway. “The formal evaluation is in two hours. The demon lords will be watching. The Church will be watching. Seraphina will be watching.” She glanced back, her red eyes meeting his. “Don’t disappoint me, Emperor. I’ve already watched you do that once. I don’t think I can bear to see it again.”

She was gone before he could respond, the door closing behind her with a soft click.

Rin exhaled loudly, lowering her dagger. “Well. That was terrifying.”

Kaelen stood motionless, her words echoing in his head. Power doesn’t change people. It just shows them what they already are.

“Kaelen?” Rin’s hand touched his arm, her voice uncharacteristically soft. “Hey. You’re doing that thing where you go somewhere else. Come back.”

He blinked, focusing on her face. “I’m here.”

“Are you?” She searched his eyes, her orange gaze intent. “Because that woman—she’s not here to evaluate you. She’s here to decide if you’re worth forgiving. And I don’t think she’s planning to say yes.”
