The silence that followed Kaelen’s display was not peaceful. It was the silence of a held breath, of a hundred hearts calculating, of the fragile architecture of the Academy’s peace groaning under a new weight.

The demon girl with the ram’s horns was released. The injured Church cadet was carried to the healers. Students dispersed in clusters, their whispers sharp as broken glass. And through it all, Kaelen stood at the center of the arena, Seraphina’s hand still clasped around his, feeling the seal thrum like a plucked harp string.

I showed too much, he thought, watching the Church cadets retreat with looks that ranged from fear to hatred to something more clinical. But hiding was no longer an option.

“You’re an idiot,” Seraphina murmured beside him, her voice light but her grip iron. “A magnificent, reckless idiot.”
