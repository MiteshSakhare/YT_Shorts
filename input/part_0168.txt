# Part 22: The Gathering Dark

NARRATOR:
It carefully. The script was ancient, the language a dialect he hadn’t spoken in three hundred years. But the words were clear:

And in the binding of the Emperor’s soul, there shall be a price. Not for his freedom, but for his return. The darkness he carried shall be split, half to bind, half to wander. And the wandering half shall seek its other until the world is remade or unmade.

He read it twice, three times, the words burning into his memory. “The darkness was split. Half in the seal. Half…”

“Half was set free,” Vex’ahlia finished. “It’s been wandering for three hundred years, looking for a way back. Valerius was its latest vessel. But it’s had others. Wars. Plagues. The fall of your empire. Every catastrophe that’s marked the last three centuries—the darkness was there, feeding, growing.”

Kaelen’s hands tightened on the scroll. “And now it’s inside me.”

“The half that was in Valerius is. But the other half—the half that was already in you—is still there. The seal didn’t just bind your power. It bound the darkness with it. When you broke the seal, you let it all back in.” She turned to face him fully. “You’re carrying something that was never meant to be contained in one body. Not even yours.”

The darkness in his chest stirred, as if responding to her words. He pressed his hand against it, feeling it pulse like a second heartbeat.

“What happens if I can’t hold it?”
