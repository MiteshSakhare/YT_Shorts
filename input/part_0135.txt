# Part 18: The Weight of Peace

NARRATOR:
“A crown?” Rin’s voice was sharp.

Seraphina didn’t answer. She didn’t have to.

Kaelen looked at the maps, at the fragile lines that divided human from demon territory, at the empty space where the Academy sat like a bridge between worlds. “I don’t want a crown.”

“The prophecy says you’re the twice-crowned king,” Elara said quietly. “Maybe that’s what it meant. One crown from the past. One from the future.”

“Or maybe it means something else.” He stood, walking to the window, looking out at the borderlands where the army had been. “I’m not going to let them put me on a throne. I’m not going to be anyone’s symbol. If we’re going to build something new, it has to be built by all of us. Not by one king.”

Seraphina came up behind him, her arms wrapping around his waist. “They won’t like that.”

“They’ll have to learn.”

Rin bounced off the cushions, landing beside him. “I like it. No crown means no one can take it from you.” She grinned. “Also, crowns are heavy. I tried one once. Gave me a headache.”

Elara laughed, the sound surprising all of them. “I’d like to hear that story.”

“Later.” Rin hooked her arm through Kaelen’s. “Right now, I’m hungry. And since the army’s gone, maybe we can actually eat something that wasn’t rationed for a siege.”

Kaelen looked at the three of them—Seraphina pressed against his back, Rin at his side, Elara gathering her books with a smile—and felt something loosen in his chest. Something that had been tight since the moment he woke in this body.
