# Part 10: The Ghost of an Empire

NARRATOR:
Rin made a sound low in her throat, a warning growl. Kaelen raised a hand, silencing her.

“You came to evaluate me,” he said. “So evaluate.”

Morwen’s smile faded. She walked around the bed, her movements slow, deliberate, as if she was giving him time to prepare for something he couldn’t anticipate. When she stopped, she was close enough to touch, close enough that he could smell the incense that clung to her robes—the same incense that had burned in the temples of his old empire.

“I served your empire for two hundred years,” she said quietly. “I was there when you conquered the Crystal Court. I was there when you burned the Sunken City. I was there when you made the pact with my mistress—Seraphina’s great-grandmother. I stood in the shadows and watched you sell your soul for a second chance, and then I watched you run from the price.”

She reached out and touched the collar, her fingers tracing the silver rings. “You left her in chains. You left me in chains. Three hundred years, waiting for a king who was too much of a coward to come back and face what he’d done.”

Her voice cracked on the last words, and for a moment, Kaelen saw something beneath the mask—not hatred, but a grief so old it had become part of her bones.

“I didn’t remember,” he said. “When I woke in this body, the memories of my last days were gone. The pact. The vow. Her.” He met her red eyes. “I’m not making excuses. I’m telling you the truth.”
