# Part 20: The Serpent's Return

NARRATOR:
Felt it pulse in answer, and thought of the people waiting for him below. Seraphina, with her fierce love and her three hundred years of patience. Rin, with her hunger and her loyalty and her strange, fierce tenderness. Elara, with her books and her questions and her unwavering faith in a future she was helping to build.

They were the reason he had saved Valerius. They were the reason he had chosen mercy over vengeance, hope over fear. They were the reason he was becoming something new.

He turned from the walls and walked back toward the light, the darkness in his chest quieting, the silver ring on his finger warm against his skin.

Tomorrow, there would be work to do. Tomorrow, the council would debate, and Valerius would face his trial, and the followers of the old war would gather in the shadows. But tonight, there was warmth, and there was peace, and there were people who had chosen to stand beside him.

He walked through the gates of the Academy and into the arms of the women who loved him, and for the first time in three hundred years, he let himself believe that maybe—just maybe—he had found something worth keeping.
